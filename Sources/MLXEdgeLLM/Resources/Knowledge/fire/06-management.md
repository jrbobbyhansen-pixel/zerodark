# Fire Lays, Fuel, and Management

**Fire lays by purpose:**
- **Teepee:** Tinder in center, kindling leaning in teepee shape. Quick heat and flame. Burns fast. Good for signaling, boiling, initial warmth.
- **Log cabin:** Pairs of logs stacked perpendicular, teepee inside. Long sustained burn. Good for overnight warmth and large cooking.
- **Star fire:** 4–5 logs laid spoke-pattern from center. Push inward as they burn. Minimum fuel use. Good for extended operation.
- **Keyhole:** Horseshoe of rocks, fire burns at back, coals raked forward into flat keyhole for cooking. Separates flames from cooking area.
- **Dakota hole:** Two interconnected holes — fire in one, air intake from other. Concealed, wind-independent, hot. Excellent tactical fire.

**Fuel progression:** Never skip stages.
1. Tinder: ignites from spark/coal (pencil-thin and smaller, dry)
2. Kindling: sustained from tinder (finger to thumb thickness)
3. Fuel: sustained from kindling (wrist to arm thickness)
4. Main fuel: sustained burn (wrist-thickness logs or larger)

**Wet fuel drying:** Prop wet wood near but not in fire for 20+ minutes before adding. Spit sizzling from wood = still wet.

**Coal temperature reading:** White ash layer over orange glow = ideal cooking coal. All white ash = too hot. Black and cold = restart.

**Fire suppression:** Smother with dirt or sand (preferred). Water works but create steam risk in enclosed spaces. Separate logs before smothering.

**Tactical fire discipline (Dakota hole):** Invisible flame, minimal smoke, directional heat. Cover when not in use.
