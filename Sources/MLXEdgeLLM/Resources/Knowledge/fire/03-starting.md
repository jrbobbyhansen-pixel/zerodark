# Fire Starting – Every Method

## Bow Drill (Friction)

- **Spindle**: 18in dry softwood (cedar, willow, yucca, mullein) – straight, dry, no knots
- **Fireboard**: same wood species, dry, notch cut to edge of depression, leaf under notch to catch coal
- **Technique**: steady downward body weight pressure, fast horizontal strokes. Smoke is normal. Coal = orange glow that persists after stopping
- **Transfer coal to tinder bundle**, fold bundle around coal, blow gently until flame

## Ferrocerium/Flint and Steel

Hold rod at 45° into tinder bundle. Sharp downward scrape toward tinder. Goal: sparks landing IN tinder, not flying past.

## Lens

Magnifying glass, eyeglasses, clear bag of water, shaped ice. Focus point must be small and sharp. Wait for ignition, not just smoke.

## Battery + Fine Steel Wool

Touch 0000 steel wool to both terminals of 9V battery. Ignites immediately.

## Tinder Quality (Best to Worst)

Birch bark – dry fine grass – cattail fluff – shredded cedar bark – dried fungi – pine needles

## Wet Conditions

Split wet wood with rock to expose dry interior. Use resinous heartwood of dead pine. Gather dry tinder under dense conifers.
