# Fire Starting — Every Reliable Method

Fire provides warmth, water purification, signaling, cooking, light, and psychological stability. Master multiple methods.

**Bow drill friction fire:**
Spindle: 18in dry softwood (cedar, willow, yucca, mullein), straight, no knots, tapered to blunt point. Fireboard: same or similar species, dry, flat surface, depression carved 3/4in from edge with notch cut to edge (notch captures coal). Bow: 24in curved branch, paracord or shoelace. Handhold: smooth hardwood or bark to create downward pressure. Technique: long fast horizontal strokes with downward body weight on spindle. Smoke starts early — continue until heavy, black smoke, then 10 more full strokes. Transfer glowing coal on leaf to tinder bundle. Fold and blow gently.

**Flint and steel / ferrocerium:**
Hold ferrocerium rod at 45° angle with tip buried in tinder bundle. Strike sharp downward scrape with spine of knife or striker. Goal: sparks landing IN tinder, not flying away. Flint: strike downward glancing blow to create sparks. Best tinder: char cloth, fungus amadou, fine dry grass.

**Lens:**
Magnifying glass, eyeglasses (convex lenses only), clear plastic bag filled with clear water, polished spoon bottom, shaped ice. Focus sunlight to smallest possible point — must be smaller than pencil point for reliable ignition. Works only in direct sunlight.

**Battery methods:**
9V battery + fine (0000) steel wool: touch both terminals simultaneously. Ignites in 1–2 seconds. AA/AAA with wire: steel wool bridging both terminals of two batteries in series. Reliable when steel wool is dry.

**Matches/lighter:**
If available, use sparingly. Store backup lighter in waterproof container. Windproof lighters in wet/windy conditions.

**Tinder quality (best to worst):** Char cloth — amadou fungus — birch bark — dry fine grass — cattail fluff — shredded cedar bark — pine needles — dry leaves

**Wet conditions strategy:** Split wet wood with rock to expose dry interior. Use resinous heartwood of dead pine. Gather dry tinder under dense conifers. Dry tinder in armpit or next to skin before use.
