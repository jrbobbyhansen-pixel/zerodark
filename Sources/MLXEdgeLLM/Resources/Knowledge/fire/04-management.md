# Fire Management – Lays, Fuel, Control

## Fire Lays

- **Teepee**: quick heat, good for boiling, burns fast
- **Log cabin**: long sustained burn, good for signaling
- **Star fire**: push logs inward as they burn, minimal fuel, good for overnight
- **Keyhole**: rocks in horseshoe, cooking coals raked into flat section

## Fuel Progression

Tinder (pencil-thin, lights easily) – kindling (finger to wrist thickness) – fuel logs. Never skip a stage.

## Wet Fuel

Prop wet wood near fire 20+ minutes before adding.

## Signal Fire

Add green wet vegetation to existing fire – white smoke visible miles in daylight. Rubber/oil/plastic – black smoke.

## Suppression

Smother with dirt or sand. Water works but use carefully – steam risk in enclosed spaces. Separate burning logs first.

## Coal Cooking Temperature

Orange glow with white ash layer = correct. All white ash = too hot. Black = not enough heat.
