# Fire for Signaling

**Ground-to-air signals:**
Three fires in a triangle (equilateral, 100ft separation) = universal distress signal. Single fire with strong smoke = distress. Fire on elevated ground visible farther.

**Smoke types:**
Green wet vegetation on fire — white smoke (visible against dark forest, trees). Rubber, plastic, oil, tires — black smoke (visible against sky, open terrain). Choose contrast to background.

**Signal fire construction:**
Prepare fuel in advance so signal fire can be lit instantly when aircraft heard. Pre-position kindling and fuel. Cover with waterproof material in rain. Have ignition method ready (dry, accessible).

**Smoke during day vs fire at night:**
Day: smoke more visible than flame. Night: flame more visible than smoke. Adjust accordingly. Have both prepared.

**Mirror signals:**
Aim reflection toward aircraft. V-shape with fingers at arm's length — aim sunlight point through V to target. Visible 10+ miles in clear air. Works even on overcast days (diffuse light).

**Ground symbols (10ft+ in size, high contrast):**
- X = need medical help
- V = need assistance
- ↑ = traveling this direction
- ↑↑ = all clear
Use rocks, logs, trampled vegetation, earth disturbance. Make as large as possible.
