# Food Processing and Preservation in the Field

**Jerky (dried meat):**
Slice meat 1/4in thick with grain. Hang or lay on rocks in sun with airflow. Full sun and airflow: 1–2 days. Smoke helps preservation and flavor. Meat should be leather-dry and bend without breaking before it's done. Properly dried jerky lasts 2+ weeks without refrigeration.

**Smoking:**
Build small fire with hardwood (hickory, apple, maple). Hang meat 18–24in above smoldering coals. Smoke without flame 8–12 hours. Both hot smoking (partially cooks) and cold smoking (below 100°F, preserves only) work. Smoked meat lasts significantly longer than fresh.

**Caching:**
Wrap dried meat in green leaves and clay to exclude insects and moisture. Bury in cool dry location. Mark cache location from multiple reference points. Meat cached in cold weather (below 40°F) stays fresh 1–2 weeks.

**Rendering fat:**
Fat is the most calorie-dense survival food. Cut into small pieces. Heat slowly in container. Liquid fat separates from solids (cracklings). Pour off liquid fat, store. Rendered fat lasts weeks in cool conditions. Pemmican equivalent: dried meat powder mixed with rendered fat and berries.

**Plant preservation:**
Drying is most reliable. Slice thin, sun dry on rocks. Berries: press into leather and dry. Roots: slice and dry. Dried plants last months.

**Prioritizing food processing time vs benefit:**
Only process when at temporary or semi-permanent camp. On the move: eat raw what is safe raw, boil everything else in a single pot. Preservation becomes priority at Day 3+ of stationary position.
