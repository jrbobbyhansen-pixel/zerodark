# Nutritional Priorities in Survival

**Caloric density ranking (per 100g):**
Rendered fat: 900cal | Nuts/seeds: 600–700cal | Dried meat: 250–350cal | Grains: 350cal | Cooked meat: 200–250cal | Root vegetables: 80–150cal | Leafy greens: 20–40cal

**Survival math:**
Average adult needs 2,000–2,500cal/day at rest. Heavy exertion: 4,000–5,000cal. 3,500cal deficit = approximately 1lb fat burned. Body fat is survival reserve – lean person with 15% body fat = 24,000cal reserve = ~10 days without food at rest.

**Starvation timeline:**
Day 1–2: glycogen depleted, hunger peaks. Day 3–5: ketosis begins, hunger decreases, mental clarity may decline. Week 1–2: body adapts, performance decremented but functional. Week 3+: muscle catabolism accelerates, critical organ function at risk. Death typically at 30–40% body weight loss.

**Macronutrient priorities:**
1. Carbohydrates: fast energy, brain fuel (roots, seeds, berries)
2. Protein: muscle maintenance, healing (meat, insects, eggs, fish)
3. Fats: sustained energy, warmth, fat-soluble vitamins (rendered fat, nuts, fatty meat)

**Micronutrient deficiencies (longer-term survival):**
- Vitamin C (scurvy): pine needle tea, rose hips, fresh berries, raw meat – prevent after 2 weeks
- Salt: critical for fluid regulation – pine ash contains some sodium, meat contains sodium
- Iodine: coastal seaweeds provide iodine – inland deficiency risk after weeks

**Eat or evade:**
In an active evasion situation, caloric need increases 50–100%. Prioritize calorie-dense portable foods. Accept stomach discomfort from unusual foods. The alternative (weakness from hunger) is worse.
