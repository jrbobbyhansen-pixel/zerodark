# Hunting and Trapping

**Figure-4 deadfall:**
Three sticks. Vertical support with notch at top. Diagonal brace with notch at mid-point. Horizontal trigger with notch at far end connecting to bait. Heavy flat rock balanced on diagonal. Animal disturbs bait – trigger releases – rock falls. Set on runs and trails with sign. Effective for rabbit, squirrel, groundhog. Bait: peanut butter, seeds, berries.

**Snare:**
28-gauge wire (commercial snare wire, or stripped copper wire). Loop diameter: fist-sized for rabbit, golf ball for squirrel. Set loop at nose height: 3–4in off ground for rabbit, 2in for squirrel. Set on active trail confirmed by tracks, scat, worn vegetation. Anchor to driven stake or bent sapling. Wrap extra wire around anchor point.

**Spring snare (self-setting):**
Snare on bent sapling. Trigger stick holds sapling bent. Animal triggers – sapling snaps up – catches animal off ground. Prevents escape and keeps catch from other predators.

**Spear:**
Straight hardwood 6–8ft. Sharpen to 4-sided point (stronger than cone). Fire harden (rotate in dry heat ABOVE fire, not in flames – dry heat, not charring).

**Set location:**
Check runs daily. Move sets that show no activity after 48 hours. Wear gloves to reduce scent. Urinate away from set area. Use natural materials for camouflage.

**Processing:** Gut immediately (remove organs before body temperature drops). Keep heart and liver – high nutrition. Boil organ meat first. Skin before or after gutting (personal preference). Remove all fat from carnivores (may contain toxins). Cook all wild game to 165°F equivalent (no pink, juices run clear).
