# Plant Foraging – Safe Identification

**Universal edibility test (in order – skip if plant shows any fail indicator):**
1. Identify: Is it in the always-toxic list? Stop immediately if yes.
2. Smell: Unpleasant, bitter almond, or chemical = stop
3. Skin: Rub small amount on inner wrist, wait 15 minutes – rash, burning, blistering = stop
4. Lips: Touch to lips, wait 3 minutes – tingling, numbness, burning = stop
5. Tongue: Small piece on tongue, 15 minutes – reaction = stop
6. Swallow: Small amount, wait 8 hours – any reaction = stop
7. Eat: Small portion only after passing all stages

**Always toxic – never test:**
- Umbrella-shaped white flower clusters (hemlock, water hemlock, poison hemlock)
- Plants with shiny three-leaf pattern (poison ivy, poison oak)
- Plants with milky white sap (most toxic, some exceptions)
- Red or white berries (70%+ of these are toxic – avoid unless positively identified)
- Bitter almond smell (hydrogen cyanide)
- Pods/beans with kidney/bean shape (many fatal)

**Always safe (no test needed):**
- Dandelion: entire plant edible – leaves (bitter), flowers (tea), roots (roasted)
- Cattail: pollen (flour), young green flower head (cook like corn), starchy root (year-round), shoots (raw like cucumber)
- Pine: inner bark (spring, nutritious), needles (tea, vitamin C), seeds
- Acorns: all oak acorns edible after leaching (soak 24hr, change water, repeat 2–3 times)
- Plantain weed (not banana): ground-cover leaf plant, raw or cooked
- Clover: all parts edible
- Blackberries, raspberries, strawberries: aggregate berries almost always safe

**Caloric value:** Acorns 387cal/100g. Pine nuts 673cal/100g. Cattail root 60cal/100g (large volume possible).
