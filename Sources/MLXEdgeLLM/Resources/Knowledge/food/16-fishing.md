# Field Fishing Without Gear

**Improvised hook:** Bent wire (any gauge), bent safety pin, carved bone or hardwood with barb, large thorn with groove for line.

**Line:** Paracord inner strands (7 strands per cord), twisted plant fiber (cattail, stinging nettle), sinew from processed hide, monofilament from electronics.

**Bait:** Earthworms (under rocks in moist soil), insects, beetle larvae, crayfish tails, liver scraps, berries, shiny cloth or foil as lure.

**Refraction rule:** Aim below where fish appears. Water refracts light making fish appear higher than actual position. Aim 1–2 body lengths below apparent position.

**Spear fishing:** Best in clear, shallow moving water. Stand still – fish become accustomed to presence. Strike when fish is within 12in. 5–6ft spear, multiple tines more effective than single.

**Fish trap (weir):** Stack rocks across stream leaving small opening with funnel shape pointing into trap. Fish swim upstream into narrow exit, cannot find large opening to exit. Check every 2–4 hours.

**Cone trap:** Woven branches or wire formed into cone with narrow inward-facing entrance. Weight with rock, bait inside. Leave in stream overnight.

**Gorge hook:** Straight sharpened twig or bone with line attached at center. When fish swallows bait, pulling line causes gorge to orient sideways, lodging in throat.

**All fish:** Cook thoroughly. Fish cooking: flesh flakes and separates easily when poked with stick. Internal flesh opaque throughout. Guts removed before cooking (or during cleaning). Gills and scales removed or left intact for ember cooking. Avoid raw freshwater fish (parasites including tapeworm).
