# Plant Foraging – Safe Identification and Edibility Test

## Universal Edibility Test Procedure

1. Smell – unpleasant = stop
2. Rub on inner wrist – wait 15 minutes, rash = stop
3. Touch to lips – wait 3 minutes, burning/numbness = stop
4. Place small piece on tongue – 15 minutes, reaction = stop
5. Chew small amount, do not swallow – 8 hours monitoring
6. Swallow small amount – wait 8 more hours before eating more

## NEVER Test

Mushrooms, umbrella-shaped white flowers (hemlock family), shiny leaves, milky white sap, red or white berries, bitter almond smell, three-leaf patterns (poison ivy/oak).

## Always-Safe Plants (No Test Needed)

- **Dandelion**: entire plant edible (leaves, root, flowers)
- **Cattail**: pollen – green flower head – starchy root (all seasons)
- **Pine needles**: vitamin C tea, inner bark edible in spring
- **Acorns**: leach tannins first (soak 24 hours, change water, repeat twice)
- **Plantain weed**: ground-cover leaves, raw or cooked
- **Blackberries/raspberries**: aggregate berries almost always safe
- **Clover**: all parts

## Nutritional Value Per 100g

Acorns 387cal. Pine nuts 673cal. Cattail pollen 340cal.
