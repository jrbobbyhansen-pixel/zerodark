# Plant Toxicity – Critical Identification

**Immediately fatal plants (DO NOT test):**
- **Water hemlock** (Cicuta): most violently toxic plant in North America. Carrot-like, hollow chambered roots, umbrella flower. All parts. Kills within hours.
- **Poison hemlock** (Conium): musty odor, purple-blotched stems, umbrella flower. Killed Socrates. All parts.
- **Deadly nightshade** (Atropa): shiny black berries, 5-petaled purple flower. 2–5 berries fatal to child.
- **White snakeroot**: caused milk sickness (killed Lincoln's mother). White flower clusters, heart-shaped leaves.
- **Death camas**: looks like wild onion but NO onion smell. Bulb, grass-like leaves.
- **Monkshood/wolfsbane**: purple hood-shaped flowers. All parts. Absorbed through skin.

**Toxic but non-lethal (causes severe illness):**
- Pokeweed: purple berries (attractive), large plant. Cooked leaves sometimes eaten but risky.
- Elderberries: raw = nausea. Cooked = safe and nutritious.
- Wild parsnip: not toxic but causes severe photosensitive skin burns on contact + sunlight.

**Safe look-alikes and their dangerous counterparts:**
- Wild garlic/onion (has onion smell) vs death camas (no smell) – ALWAYS smell first
- Wild carrot (hairy stem, purple center flower) vs hemlock (hairless, purple blotched stem)
- Elderberry (safe cooked) vs water hemlock (never safe)

**Rule of three R's:**
If you're not 100% certain – **R**ead all identifying features, **R**ule out all toxic look-alikes, **R**emember the universal edibility test.
