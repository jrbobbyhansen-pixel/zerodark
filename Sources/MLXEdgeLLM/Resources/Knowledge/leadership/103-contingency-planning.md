# Contingency Planning – Branches and Sequels

**Contingency:** Plan likely deviations before they occur. Prevents reactive stress – decision already made, just execute.

**Branch:** Change plan based on anticipated circumstances or enemy action during execution. "If resistance at CP1, use Route B instead of A."

**Sequel:** Subsequent operation following current. Plan next while executing current.

**Plan for:** Vehicle breakdown. Unexpected contact. Key casualties. Comms failure. Weather change. Objective conditions differ.

**Triggers:** Specific observable conditions trigger branch execution. Unambiguous. "If vehicle disabled prior to Phase Line RED, execute Vehicle Recovery Plan." Not: "if things go bad."

**Brief subordinates:** All contingencies briefed before execution. Each person knows: trigger for each branch, their role, team response in each.

**After:** Did contingencies address what happened? Update based on execution experience.
