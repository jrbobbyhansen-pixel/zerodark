# Shelter Construction

**Priority:** Exposure kills faster than dehydration. Wind is the primary threat – 20mph wind at 32°F creates effective -4°F temperature.

## Debris Hut (Temperate Forest)

Ridge pole 9ft long, supported 3ft off ground at one end. Ribs along both sides. Pile debris minimum 3ft thick (test: armful = arm disappears). Small enough to heat with body warmth alone (arms-width wider, arms-length longer than body).

## Lean-To

Three A-frame poles lashed with clove hitch, cross poles for frame, thatch bark/leaves shingled from bottom up. Needs fire in front to reflect heat. Good for rain, poor for cold.

## Snow Quinzhee (Arctic/Winter)

Pile snow 6ft high × 10ft wide. Poke guide sticks 6in all around surface. Wait 2 hours (sintering – crystals bond). Hollow inside leaving 6in walls confirmed by sticks. Sleeping platform elevated above entrance (cold air sinks).

## Emergency

Crawl under dense conifer, pile branches over yourself.

## Ground Insulation

Always minimum 2 inches between body and ground. Body loses heat to cold ground 25× faster than to cold air.
