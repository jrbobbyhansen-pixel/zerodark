# Debris Hut Construction

Most effective all-season natural shelter for temperate forest. Can keep occupant alive at 20°F with no fire.

**Materials needed:** One straight pole 9–10ft, long branches for ribs, deep leaf/debris pile.

**Construction:**
1. Find or make a tripod anchor at head end – two short poles lashed A-frame with one horizontal – about 3ft high
2. Lay ridge pole from anchor to ground – should accommodate body length plus 2ft
3. Lay ribs along both sides of ridge pole, tight together, from ground to pole
4. Add cross hatching (smaller sticks over ribs) to hold debris
5. Pile debris (dry leaves, pine needles, dry grass) minimum 3ft thick on all sides and over top. Test: fill armful – if arm disappears when pressed in, it's thick enough. 4ft thick in cold climates.
6. Fill interior with dry debris for bedding – minimum 6in under body

**Size:** Body width + 6in per side. Body length + 1ft. Tight = warm. Too large = impossible to heat with body warmth.

**Thermal test:** Build it, get inside, close entrance with debris pile, wait 20 minutes. Should feel noticeably warmer than outside.

**Ground insulation:** Minimum 4in of dry debris under body. Ground conducts heat away 25× faster than still air.

**Entrance:** Close with debris pile from inside. Poncho or pack over entrance reduces wind infiltration.

**Maintenance:** Wet debris loses insulation value. Replace if soaked. Add fresh dry debris to compensate for compaction.

**Limitation:** No fire inside – fire risk. Debris hut is fire-free shelter only.
