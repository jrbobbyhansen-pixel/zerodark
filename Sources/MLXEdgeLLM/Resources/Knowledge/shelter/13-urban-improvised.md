# Urban Improvised Shelter

Urban environments offer building materials but present security and chemical risks.

**Building selection:**
Structural integrity first – check for foundation cracks, leaning walls, fire damage. Multi-story: use upper floors for observation, first floor has best structural stability. Avoid basement in flood-prone areas. Ground floor is most accessible but least defensible.

**Rapid structural assessment:**
Cracks running diagonally from door/window corners = structural failure risk. Floors sagging = compromised joists. Exterior walls leaning = imminent collapse. Do not enter visibly compromised structures.

**Concealment setup:**
- Cover all windows and light emitting points before using artificial light
- Do not cook on upper floors with open windows (heat and smell signature)
- Multiple entry/exit points identified before occupation
- Establish fields of observation from each window before settling

**Improvised insulation:**
Cardboard in layers = effective insulation. 4 layers of cardboard equals wool blanket in insulation value. Paper, fabric, plastic sheeting all useful. Stuffing clothing with newspaper or leaves adds warmth.

**Fire safety:**
Urban buildings may contain chemicals, asbestos, lead paint. Ventilate thoroughly before occupation. No open fires inside – structural fire risk. Use outdoor or improvised contained stove with chimney through window.

**Water:**
Hot water heater tanks in structures contain potable water even after grid failure (50–80 gallons). Toilet tanks (not bowls) contain potable water. Pipes may retain pressurized water for 24–48 hours after supply failure.
