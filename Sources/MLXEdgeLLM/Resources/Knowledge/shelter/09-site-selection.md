# Shelter Site Selection

The wrong location kills regardless of shelter quality.

**Avoid:**
- Valley bottoms (cold air drains down at night – 10–20°F colder, flooding risk, insect concentration)
- Hilltops (wind, lightning exposure, thermal signature visible)
- Single tall trees (lightning rods)
- Dry riverbeds (flash flood risk – wall of water arrives without warning)
- South-facing slopes in summer (overheating), north-facing in winter (coldest)
- Dense brush (insects, predators, poor observation)

**Seek:**
- Natural windbreaks: ridge flanks, dense thickets, terrain folds
- East or southeast facing: morning sun warms sleeping area, afternoon shade
- Natural drainage away from site: build on slight rise if possible
- Materials proximity: debris, wood, water within 100m
- 100–200ft above valley floor: out of cold air drainage, protected from wind, observation advantage

**Tactical considerations (BLISS):**
- **B**lend in – matches surrounding terrain
- **L**ow silhouette – doesn't break skyline
- **I**rregular shape – no straight lines visible from outside
- **S**mall – minimum size to retain heat, minimum observable signature
- **S**ecluded – not on trail, road, or natural gathering point

**Overhead hazard:** Check for dead branches (widow makers) before building under any tree. One branch can kill.

**Animal signs:** Avoid siting near active animal trails, burrows, or obvious feeding areas. Predator risk and pest risk both elevated.

**Two withdrawal routes:** Always. One primary, one alternate. Know them before occupation.
