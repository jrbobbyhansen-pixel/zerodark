# Snow and Arctic Shelters

Snow is an excellent insulator. Inside a properly built snow shelter: 15–32°F regardless of outside temperature.

**Quinzhee:**
1. Pile snow into mound 6ft high × 10–12ft wide
2. Poke guide sticks 6in all around surface (these control wall thickness during hollowing)
3. Wait minimum 2 hours – sintering bonds snow crystals, structure becomes rigid
4. Hollow from one end leaving 6in walls (confirmed by sticks)
5. Sleeping platform elevated above entrance – cold air sinks to lowest point
6. Poke ventilation hole through wall with stick – critical to prevent CO2 buildup
7. Mark vent hole with stick from outside so it can be found and cleared if blocked by new snow

**Snow trench:**
Fastest option. Dig body-length trench 2ft wide, 3ft deep. Cover with skis, branches, tarp, snow blocks. Line floor with insulation. Entrance lower than sleeping area.

**Igloo (advanced):**
Cut snow blocks 2ft × 2ft × 6in from consolidated snow. Stack in spiral pattern leaning inward. Final keystone block locked in from inside. Time: 1–2 hours for experienced person. Superior but requires proper snow type and technique.

**Critical rules for all snow shelters:**
- Poke ventilation hole before sleeping – CO/CO2 buildup kills
- Mark entrance and vent from outside (can be buried by new snow)
- Candle or body heat sufficient for interior warmth – no fires
- Check vent hourly in heavy snowfall
- Water dripping from ceiling = temperature too warm – scrape ice buildup
