# Tarp and Improvised Shelter Construction

**Tarp configurations (with any 8×10ft sheet):**

**A-frame:** Ridge line between two trees, tarp draped over and staked out both sides. Excellent rain protection. Add door panel staked out for wind.

**Lean-to:** One side staked high (back wall), front open toward fire. Quick, simple, good wind protection when oriented correctly. Face opening away from prevailing wind.

**Plow point:** One corner staked high, opposite corner staked to ground. Both sides drape and stake. Diamond orientation. Good in wind – sheds rain off all sides.

**Wedge:** Front staked high, rear corner staked to ground. Full coverage including entrance. Best for prolonged rain.

**Ridgeline tension:** Use truckers hitch (creates mechanical advantage) to get ridgeline taut. Taut ridgeline sheds rain; slack sags and pools water.

**Improvised without tarp:**
- **Poncho lean-to:** Two ponchos buttoned together. Tie grommet corners to trees at 45° angle.
- **Space blanket:** Reflective surface facing inward reflects body heat back. Secure all edges from wind – tears easily.
- **Bark panels:** Peel large sheets of birch or cedar bark. Shingle bottom-up (like roofing tiles) on pole frame.
- **Leaves on frame:** Debris lean-to with thatch. Layer leaves bottom-up, overlap minimum 6in.

**Stake alternatives:** Forked sticks, rocks, logs. Drive improvised stakes at 45° angle away from tension direction.

**Wind and rain orientation:** Back to prevailing wind. Entrance should face away from dominant weather direction. Check with wet finger before committing.
