# Escape From Restraints

**Zip tie (forward):** Raise arms, bring down hard against stomach, pull elbows out. Friction and force break ratchet. Or: Rough surface, saw binding against it (30–60 sec).

**Zip tie (behind back):** Step feet through to bring to front, then use above method. Flex hands wider while being bound – creates slack. Get ties across wrist bones.

**Flex cuffs:** Same break as zip ties. Sawing more reliable.

**Rope:** While being bound: tense muscles, flex outward, take deep breath to expand wrists. Creates 0.5–1cm slack when relaxed. Rope loosens with moisture. Work knots with fingers.

**Metal handcuffs:** Shimming: flat metal (bobby pin) inserted between ratchet and teeth. Push in teeth direction. Work both sides. Double lock requires key or pick.

**Improvised key:** Standard cuffs share common key pattern. Bobby pin bent to shape. Study key profile, replicate.

**Important:** Remain calm. Rushed attempts alert captors, result in tighter restraint.
