# Compass Navigation – Fundamentals

**Parts:** Baseplate (with ruler), rotating bezel (with degree markings 0–360), magnetic needle (red end points north), direction of travel arrow, index mark.

**Taking a bearing to a destination:**
1. Point direction of travel arrow at destination
2. Rotate bezel until N aligns with red end of needle
3. Read bearing at index mark
4. Walk in direction of travel arrow maintaining bearing

**Following a bearing:**
Rotate bezel to desired bearing. Turn entire body until red needle aligns with N on bezel. Walk in direction of travel arrow. Check bearing every 100m (every 100 paces).

**Back azimuth:** Add 180° to bearing (subtract if over 360°) to find direction back to start. Essential for returning.

**Magnetic declination:** Difference between magnetic north (needle) and true north (maps). Eastern US: 10–15° west declination (subtract from magnetic bearing to get true). Western US: up to 20° east (add). Check local declination and adjust all map work.

**Resection (finding your position):**
Identify two known landmarks visible and on map. Take bearing to each. Convert to back azimuths. Draw lines from each landmark on map. Intersection = your position.

**Triangulation:** Same as resection with 3 landmarks – reduces error.

**Pace counting:** Count double steps (each time right foot strikes). Calibrate on known 100m course – typical count is 60–68. Use tally counter or tie knots in cord. Every 1,000 paces = approximately 1km (adjust for your calibration).
