# Urban Navigation

**Grid navigation:** Most cities are grid-based. Learn grid pattern before entering. Number streets run one direction, named or lettered streets perpendicular. Count blocks as rough distance (average US city block = 80m).

**Landmark navigation:**
Identify 3–5 prominent landmarks visible from multiple points: church steeples, water towers, tall buildings, bridges. Use as reference points to maintain orientation.

**Sun in urban canyons:**
Note which side of streets receive morning vs afternoon sun. Morning sun (east) = sun on east-facing building faces. Maintain this orientation reference.

**Dead reckoning in urban:**
Step count between blocks. Note turns (left = subtract 90°, right = add 90° from current bearing). Sketch rough map of route traveled.

**Route selection:**
Avoid bottlenecks (bridges, underpasses, tunnels) when possible – single points of failure. Select parallel streets. Know alternate routes to every destination.

**Navigation when compromised:**
If disoriented in urban: find elevated position (parking structure, bridge) for overview. Find running water (flows to low ground, toward river, toward exit). Commercial signage often indicates direction of commerce/transit.

**Building numbering:**
In US cities: numbers increase away from baseline (river, main street). Even numbers typically on right/south/east, odd on left/north/west of a street.
