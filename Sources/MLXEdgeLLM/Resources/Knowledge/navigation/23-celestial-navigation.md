# Navigation Without Compass – Celestial Methods

**Sun navigation:**
- Sunrise = east (within 25°). Sunset = west (within 25°).
- Solar noon: sun is due south in northern hemisphere (north of Tropic of Cancer). Highest point in sky = due south.
- Shadow stick method: place 3ft stick vertical in ground. Mark tip of shadow. Wait 10–15 minutes. Mark tip again. Line connecting both marks runs east-west (first mark = west, second mark = east). Perpendicular to this line = north-south.

**Moon navigation:**
- If moon rises before midnight: illuminated right side = south
- If moon rises after midnight: illuminated left side = south
- Crescent moon: draw line through tips of crescent down to horizon = south

**Star navigation (northern hemisphere):**
- North Star (Polaris): end of Little Dipper handle. Always within 1° of true north. Never rises or sets. Find Cassiopeia (W-shape) – North Star is between Big Dipper and Cassiopeia on same line as Cassiopeia's middle point.
- Southern Cross (southern hemisphere): long axis of cross points toward south celestial pole (approximately south).

**Watch method:**
Analog watch: point hour hand at sun. South is halfway between hour hand and 12. (In southern hemisphere: point 12 at sun, north is halfway between 12 and hour hand.)

**Accuracy notes:** Sun and moon methods are ±15–25°. Stars are ±2–3°. Use for general direction only when compass unavailable – not for precision navigation.
