# React to Indirect Fire

Indirect fire (mortars, artillery, rockets) is the leading killer in conventional combat.

**Immediate response (first 3 seconds):**
MOVE. Do not freeze. Movement is survival. Direction: perpendicular to fall of shot line, away from impact area.
"INCOMING!" shout to alert others.

**Direction of movement:**
Move perpendicular to the line between impacts (if multiple rounds observed). Move away from open ground toward defilade (ground that blocks rounds). 90° to fall of shot direction.

**Seeking cover:**
Minimum: behind solid terrain feature (rock, earth berm, concrete). If building: interior rooms, lower floors. Prone in ditch is better than standing in open.
Do NOT: group together (bunching increases casualties), stand in open, stop moving until under cover.

**After first impacts:**
Account for personnel. Treat casualties. Attempt to identify direction of fire (sound of tube, smoke, round track). Report.

**Adjustments:**
Artillery "walks" impacts toward targets. If first round misses, second will be adjusted. Move away from adjustment direction.

**Counter-mortar:**
Mortars have short range-to-travel time (30–90 seconds typically). Movement in that window can place you outside adjusted range.
