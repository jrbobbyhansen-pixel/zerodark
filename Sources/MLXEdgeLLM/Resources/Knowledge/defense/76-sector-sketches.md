# Sector Sketches and Fire Control

**Purpose:** Sector sketch documents each fighting position's observation capabilities, fields of fire, and dead ground. Used for planning and coordination.

**Components:**
Position location: grid, sketch of position.
Primary sector: arc of fire assigned (from azimuth to azimuth).
Secondary sector: secondary responsibility.
Principal direction of fire (PDF): single most important direction.
Terrain features: visible features within sector, with distance.
Dead ground: terrain that cannot be observed or engaged (dips, reverse slopes, ravines) – mark clearly.
Observation limits: maximum effective range of weapons.

**Fire control:**
Targets: numbered reference points allowing quick, accurate fire direction. "Target 3" = everyone knows exactly where that is.
Trigger lines: line at which fire is initiated (don't fire until enemy crosses trigger line – maximizes surprise and range advantage).
Sector limits: clearly defined left and right boundary. No firing beyond sector limits (friendly fire risk).

**Coordinating fire:**
Ensure no gaps between adjacent position sectors. Sectors should overlap by minimum 10%.
Dead ground in one sector covered by fire from adjacent position.

**Machine gun fire plan:**
Final protective line (FPL): final defensive position, all weapons fire maximum rate to front. Fixed grazing fire across most likely dismounted approach.

**Coordination:**
All sector sketches compiled by leader. Gaps identified and closed. Each position leader briefs their sketch.
