# IED Recognition and Avoidance

**Indicators (any combination increases risk):**
Disturbed road surface (fresh fill, different color patch, different texture). Suspicious objects on roadway (bags, boxes, cans, tires). Dead animals on roadway (used to conceal devices). Freshly dug areas near road or path. Abandoned vehicles. Wires running from road surface to nearby cover. Triggerman (individual with phone, binoculars, or line-of-sight position not consistent with normal activity).

**High-risk areas:**
Choke points (bridges, intersections, underpasses). Routes of predictable use. Culverts. Abandoned buildings near road. Locations that canalize movement.

**If you suspect IED:**
Stop movement. Do not approach. Move off road immediately (but check for secondary device). Security 360°. Report. Await explosive ordnance disposal (EOD).
Do NOT: touch, pick up, move, or investigate suspected device. Do NOT use radio near device until minimum 300m away.

**Pattern disruption:**
Never establish predictable routes or times. Vary departure times, routes, and patterns. Brief all personnel on current IED threat and specific indicators. Report all indicators.

**Command wire detonated:**
Follow wire away from device – wire leads to triggerman. Do not cut wire (may activate device). Mark location of both device and wire.

**Pressure-plate:**
Typically buried, triggered by weight. Detectable by disturbed road surface. Minimum vehicle standoff: check road surface before driving on it.
