# Checkpoint and Traffic Control Point (TCP) Operations

**Purpose:** Control movement, screen for threats, gather intelligence on population movement.

**Physical layout:**
Serpentine approach forces vehicle slowdown. Clear standoff from initial stop point. Covered fighting positions for checkpoint personnel. Secondary fighting positions for checkpoint defense. No direct line of approach to personnel.

**Challenge procedure:**
Signal vehicle to stop at standoff distance (hand signal, visual indicator). One person approaches vehicle from rear-quarter (never directly in front). Second person provides overwatch. Third person maintains checkpoint security.

**Inspection sequence:**
Identify occupants. Purpose of travel. Documentation. Visual inspection of accessible areas. If suspicious: thorough search.

**Challenge/password:**
Challenge incoming foot personnel from covered position. Do not challenge from open. Challenge at 5m+ – close enough to identify but with time to react.

**Suspicious vehicle:**
Order vehicle to move to secondary inspection area. Maintain standoff. Do not group personnel near vehicle. If vehicle fails to comply, escalate. Use of force: escalation of force (signal, verbal, warning shot, disabling fire, lethal force) – follow ROE.

**Information collection:**
Every person at TCP is a potential intelligence source. Note patterns: who's moving, where, when, in what vehicle. Anomalies (unusual time, unusual direction, unusual cargo) worth questioning.
