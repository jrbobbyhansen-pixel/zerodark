# Obstacle Emplacement and Defense

**Purpose:** Slow, channel, or stop enemy movement to gain tactical advantage.

**Natural obstacles:**
Identify and reinforce existing terrain features: rivers, cliffs, dense vegetation, marshes. Reinforce by adding: felled trees, wire, digging to deepen or widen.

**Abatis:**
Felled trees laid with tops facing enemy. Branches left intact. Interlocked for continuous barrier. Slows vehicles and personnel. Time to construct: 2 hours per 100m with 4 personnel and cutting tools.

**Wire obstacles:**
Concertina: spiraled barbed wire, 3 coils stacked (2 bottom, 1 top). Most effective when combined with stakes. Low-wire entanglement: 3 rows of wire, 12in off ground, on stakes. Slows dismounted personnel significantly.

**Reinforcing wire:**
Use wire on likely dismounted approaches, combined with fighting positions to engage enemy struggling through wire. Wire alone does not stop – must be covered by fire.

**Anti-vehicle:**
Ditches: 2m wide, 1.5m deep. Vehicle cannot cross. Berms: earth mounded. Dragon's teeth (concrete pyramids): anti-vehicle. Log/boulder obstacles: slow and channel.

**Directing obstacles:**
Obstacles should channel enemy into killing ground – not just stop them. Obstacles with no covering fire = delay, not defeat. Obstacles that funnel into fields of fire = decisive.

**Marking friendly obstacles:**
Mark all friendly obstacles on sketch. Brief all personnel. Personnel must not move through own obstacles at night without guide.

**Improvised alert system:**
Wire strung at ankle height (trip line) connected to noise makers (cans with stones, pyrotechnic trip flares). Establishes warning perimeter beyond fighting positions. Marks boundary of defended area.
