# Vehicle-Borne IED (VBIED) Awareness

**Vehicle behaviors indicating threat:**
Low riding (heavy explosive payload). Driver appears nervous, won't make eye contact. Vehicle parked in unusual location relative to traffic pattern. Vehicle approaching checkpoint at unusual speed (accelerating or very slow). Wires, antennas, or modifications inconsistent with vehicle type. Unusual smell (fertilizer-based explosives).

**Standoff distances:**
Car bomb (50–100lbs ANFO equivalent): lethal radius 30–50m, injury radius 60–100m.
Van/truck bomb (500–1000lbs): lethal 50–75m, injury 150–250m.
Large truck (2000lbs+): lethal 100m+, injury 300m+.

**VBIED defensive measures:**
Standoff: physical barriers preventing vehicles from approaching within lethal range. Serpentine barriers: force vehicle slowdown. Jersey barriers and bollards at all critical points.

**Checkpoint procedures:**
Stop vehicles at maximum standoff. Approach from rear or angle (never directly from front – driver detonates on approach). Single vehicle at a time. Visual inspection before allowing closer.

**Warning signs in urban environment:**
Vehicles parked near crowd gathering points. Repeated circuits of same area. Vehicle occupants watching specific building or event.

**Evacuation:**
If VBIED identified before detonation: evacuate personnel to secondary protective positions. Cordon minimum 300m. Report. Do not approach.
