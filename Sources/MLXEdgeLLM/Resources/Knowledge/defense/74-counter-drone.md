# Counter-Drone Awareness and Evasion

**Commercial drone signatures:**
Visual: small (under 1m wingspan typically), low altitude (15–150m), hovering or slow movement.
Acoustic: distinctive multi-rotor buzz at 50–400Hz. Small drones: 75–125Hz. Audible at 100m+ in quiet conditions.
IR: significant heat signature from motors and batteries. Detectable by thermal optics.

**Detection:**
Listen actively, especially in open terrain. Watch for hovering or slow-moving objects. Note unnatural flight patterns (orbiting, slow back and forth = surveillance). Night: LED navigation lights visible at long range.

**Immediate counter-measures:**
Move to overhead cover immediately (under trees, bridges, buildings). Dense vegetation overhead defeats most optical sensors.
Thermal: thermal suppressive cover (emergency blanket, vegetation).
Slow deliberate movement under cover – fast movement is more detectable by optical tracking.

**Deny observation:**
Overhead camouflage netting (effective against optical, partial against thermal). Smoke (disrupts optical, IR imaging reduced). Dense canopy (defeats most commercial optical sensors).

**Electronic countermeasures:**
Commercial drones use GPS and radio control (typically 2.4GHz or 5.8GHz). GPS-denied areas (urban canyons, jamming) cause loss of control. GPS spoofing devices exist but require specialized equipment.

**Limitations:** Military-grade drones with SAR (synthetic aperture radar) and FLIR defeat most concealment. This guidance applies to commercial drones.
