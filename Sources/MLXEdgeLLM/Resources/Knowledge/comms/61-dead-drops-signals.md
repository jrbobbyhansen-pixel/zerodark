# Dead Drops and Signal Sites

**Dead drop:**
Pre-arranged location where information or material can be left for another party without direct contact. Eliminates simultaneous presence risk (surveillance detection more difficult when they don't know two parties are connected).

**Physical dead drop setup:**
Location: public but not highly surveilled. Must be accessible to both parties. Natural concealment available (rock ledge, hollow tree, loose brick).
Signal site: separate from dead drop. Presence of signal (chalk mark, stick position) indicates drop is loaded. Absence indicates empty.
Covering action: reason to be at signal site and drop location (dog walking, trash pickup, park bench).

**Container:**
Waterproof. Nondescript. Natural in that environment. Contains material wrapped to prevent deterioration.

**Operational procedure:**
Party A loads drop, signals at signal site.
Party B checks signal site first (surveillance detection). If clear, clears signal and proceeds to drop.
Party B clears signal on way out.
Party A checks signal site – cleared signal = drop received.

**Dead drop marks:**
Chalk: removable, visible, common. Tape: weatherproof, removable. Chalk mark on specific brick, tree, post. Agreed mark: X vs / vs horizontal line for different meanings.
