# Brevity Codes and Pre-Arranged Signals

**Purpose:** Communicate essential information quickly, minimize transmission time (reduces detection risk), and transmit over insecure channels without revealing operational details.

**Types:**
Single-word codes: one word = one meaning (pre-agreed). "ECHO" = meeting compromised, abort. "TANGO" = I'm safe, no surveillance.
Number codes: single digit = status. "1" = all clear. "2" = compromised. "3" = abort. "4" = urgent meeting.
Time-based: "We meet at the [number]th" – nth day of month known to both.

**Creating brevity codes:**
Simple, memorable. No obvious connection to meaning. Different for different time periods. Written on material that can be rapidly destroyed (not phone screen – can be accessed if captured).

**Challenge-response authentication:**
Challenge word is challenge half. Response word completes it. Example: Challenge "THUNDER" – correct response "STORM." Incorrect response = unknown identity.
Running password: Challenge is part of a phrase, response completes it. Change daily. Known to entire team. Challenges from 5m minimum – person who gives wrong response is threat.

**Visual signals (no-comms environment):**
Pre-agreed signals: vehicle headlight flashes, hand signals, window blind position, posted object (bucket in window = safe, no bucket = compromised).

**HAMMER acoustic transmission:**
Pre-arranged codewords transmitted via audio tones. Decode via ZeroDark app. All-environment communication when radio unavailable.
