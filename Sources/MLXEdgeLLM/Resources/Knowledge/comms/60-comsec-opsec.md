# Communications Security (COMSEC) and OPSEC

**COMSEC principles:**
No sensitive information on unsecured channels. Encryption does not grant permission to discuss everything. Cover only what needs to be covered in encrypted message – OPSEC applies to how much you communicate.

**Transmission security:**
Minimize: transmit only what is essential. Every transmission is intelligence for adversary triangulation. Time: vary transmission times, don't establish pattern. Location: change position after extended transmission. Keep it short: under 30 seconds when possible.

**Electronic emissions:**
Cell phones broadcast: tower location data, app data, advertising IDs, passive WiFi/Bluetooth scans – all without active use. IMSI catchers (stingrays) capture device IDs up to 1 mile.
Countermeasures: airplane mode when sensitive. Faraday bag. Dedicated devices per operation. Remove SIM cards.

**Operational information:**
Never state: locations, times, routes, personnel names, unit compositions on unsecured channels.
Use brevity codes, code words, time offsets (actual time ± agreed offset), grid offsets.

**Physical security:**
Destroy all written communications (burn or shred – folding/tearing is not destruction).
Assume any electronic device in sensitive meeting area is a microphone.
Check devices for tampering before operational use.

**Compartmentalization:** People know only what they need to know for their specific role. Compartmentalization limits damage from any single compromise.
