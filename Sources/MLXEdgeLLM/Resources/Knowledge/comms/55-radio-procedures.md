# Radio Procedures and Prowords

**Standard prowords:**
ROGER – message received and understood
WILCO – received, understood, and will comply
OVER – transmission complete, response expected
OUT – transmission complete, no response expected (NEVER use "OVER AND OUT" – contradictory)
BREAK – separating parts of a long message
SAY AGAIN – repeat last transmission (never "say again" unless you want them to repeat)
WAIT – short pause (under 1 minute)
WAIT OUT – pause over 1 minute
SEND YOUR TRAFFIC – proceed with your message
RADIO CHECK – request signal quality check
LIMA CHARLIE – loud and clear
CHARLIE – readable

**SITREP format:**
Situation: current status, enemy activity observed
Activity: what you're doing now
Position: current location (MGRS or description)
Future: what you plan to do next
Nothing further – end

**NET CALL:**
Station initiating: "[callsign] this is [own callsign], radio check, over."
Responding: "[callsign], [own callsign], [signal quality], over."

**Frequency management:**
Primary: main operational frequency. Alternate: backup (compromised or dead). Emergency: last resort, often distress frequency.
PACE: Primary, Alternate, Contingency, Emergency – establish all four before operation.

**Brevity:** Short transmissions. Enemy can triangulate on transmissions. Say essential only.
