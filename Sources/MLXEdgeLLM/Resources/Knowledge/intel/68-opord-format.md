# Operations Order (OPORD) – Five Paragraph Format

Standard planning format used by US military. Ensures all essential information communicated systematically.

**1. SITUATION:**
a. Enemy forces: composition, disposition, activity, capability
b. Friendly forces: higher unit mission, adjacent unit missions, support available
c. Attachments and detachments (who's with you, who's not)

**2. MISSION:**
Single sentence: WHO does WHAT, WHEN, WHERE, WHY (purpose).
Example: "Team ALPHA conducts area reconnaissance vicinity grid 14SNA0645 IAW higher commander's intent to confirm or deny enemy logistics activity NLT 2400 18MAR."

**3. EXECUTION:**
a. Commander's intent: end state desired
b. Concept of operation: how the mission will be accomplished
c. Tasks by subordinate element (each task: who, what, when, where, how)
d. Coordinating instructions: movement times, fire support, actions on contact

**4. ADMINISTRATION/LOGISTICS:**
Rations, water, ammunition, medical, equipment, vehicles.
Casualty procedures. Prisoner handling.

**5. COMMAND/SIGNAL:**
Chain of command. Where is the leader at key times.
Signals: radio frequencies, visual signals, passwords.
PACE plan.

**WARNO:** Warning order – advance notice that an order is coming. Gets people moving/packing before full OPORD issued.
