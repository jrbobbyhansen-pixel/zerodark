# Observation Post – Construction and Operation

**Site selection (KOCOA/OCOKA):**
Key terrain: any ground that gives decisive advantage
Observation and fields of fire: what can be seen and engaged from here?
Cover and concealment: both present?
Obstacles: natural barriers to approach?
Avenues of approach: can we get in and out?

**Criteria:**
Dominant ground over objective – observation priority. Cover from direct fire – at least partial. Concealment from observation – natural and man-made. Two covered withdrawal routes – primary and alternate. Communications range to base element.

**Site construction:**
Observation slit cut at ground level or in existing vegetation – do NOT cut new gaps. Overhead cover prevents aerial observation. Floor: insulation from ground for extended occupation. Materials: all natural, locally sourced, no cut vegetation from immediate area.

**OPSite discipline:**
No light, no smoke, minimal movement. Shift changes: at night, approaching from alternate direction each time. No trash accumulation. All sign policed before departure.

**Relief in place:**
New team arrives before dark. Briefed fully by departing team: what has been observed, times, activity patterns. Departing team departs only after full brief confirmed. Overlap time: minimum 30 minutes.

**Recording:** Time of observation, what was observed (SALUTE format), grid of observation, weather and light conditions. Record everything.
