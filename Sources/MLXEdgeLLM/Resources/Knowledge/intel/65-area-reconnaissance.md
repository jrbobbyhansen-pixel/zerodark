# Area Reconnaissance

**Purpose:** Collect specific information about all activities and resources in a specific area.

**Planning:**
Identify specific information requirements (what does the commander need to know?). Assign PIR (Priority Intelligence Requirements) and IR (Information Requirements). Plan routes that provide coverage of entire area. Timing: when is target activity most likely? Plan reconnaissance at that time.

**Execution:**
Observe before entering. Identify all observation positions before beginning. Establish base of operation outside area. Teams enter, observe, report, return. Minimize signature – no fires, no waste, no sign of presence.

**What to collect:**
Terrain: trafficability (can vehicles pass?), observation points, cover, obstacles. Infrastructure: bridges (estimated weight limit), roads (width, surface), water (sources, crossing points). Population: activity patterns, attitudes, information about third-party elements. Enemy: positions, routines, strength, equipment, morale indicators.

**Reporting:**
Continuous: significant activities reported immediately via radio.
Periodic: SITREP every 2–4 hours.
Final: comprehensive written report upon return.
Time-sensitive: any information that will quickly become dated reported immediately.

**Specific products:**
Sketch map of area with accurate terrain, routes, and obstacles.
Population activity timeline.
Enemy activity pattern (if applicable).
