# Route Reconnaissance

**Purpose:** Provide detailed information on a specific route for planning movement.

**Information collected:**
Road surface: type, condition, width, trafficability in rain.
Bridges: load capacity (posted or estimated), width, condition, bypass feasibility.
Chokepoints: sections where vehicles must slow or where alternatives are limited.
Observation points: elevated ground with view of route.
Covered/concealed positions alongside route.
Water sources and crossing points.
Intervisibility: from where can movement on this route be observed?

**Procedures:**
Dismount reconnaissance of all bridges before vehicle movement. Check bypass routes for all critical points. Note distances between all significant features. Time movement over entire route – provides movement planning data.

**Chokepoint analysis:**
Every bridge, tunnel, underpass, and pass is a chokepoint. For each: alternate route if blocked? Time to bypass? Observation from chokepoint into approach? Ambush feasibility?

**Route card format:**
Start point – Leg 1 (bearing, distance, terrain notes) – Waypoint 1 – Leg 2 – etc.
Include: time (estimated), terrain, hazards, alternate route, emergency rally points.

**Urban route:**
Multiple parallel routes between critical points. Every route has identified alternate. Note: one-way streets, traffic patterns, road closures. Identify covered routes (under bridges, through parking structures) for rain/drone avoidance.
