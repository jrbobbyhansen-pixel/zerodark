# HUMINT – Human Intelligence Collection

**Source development:**
Build rapport before questioning. Listen more than you speak. Establish common ground. Match communication style. Demonstrate respect. Do not appear to have an agenda.

**Questioning approaches:**
Direct: "What time did the vehicle arrive?" – works with cooperative sources.
Indirect: lead questions toward information without revealing requirement. Allows source to provide context around the answer.
Non-pertinent: unrelated questions that relax subject and establish baseline behavior.

**Source reliability assessment:**
How does the source know this? First-hand observation vs hearsay?
What is the source's position (observer vs participant)?
What is the source's motivation? (Determines potential bias.)
Has information been verified by other sources?

**Information credibility codes:**
1: Confirmed by other independent sources
2: Probably true (unconfirmed, consistent with other information)
3: Possibly true (unconfirmed, not inconsistent)
4: Doubtful (unconfirmed, inconsistent with other information)
5: Improbable (contradicted by other information)
6: Cannot be judged

**Ethics:**
Never use coercion or threats (produces unreliable information, creates enmity, illegal).
Treat all sources respectfully.
Do not make promises you cannot keep.
Compartmentalize source identity – protect sources from retaliation.
