# Pattern of Life Analysis

**Definition:** Systematic observation of an area, person, or activity over time to identify routines that enable prediction.

**Why it matters:**
Single observation = snapshot. Multiple observations at different times = pattern. Pattern = predictive capability. Predictive capability = tactical advantage.

**What to observe:**
Activity timing: When does specific activity occur? Is it regular or random?
Personnel: Who is present? At what times? Regular or changing?
Vehicles: What vehicles? Registration? Direction of travel? Times?
Routes: Which routes used consistently? At what times?
Reaction to stimulus: What triggers change in behavior? Visitors? Weather? Time of day?

**Collection methodology:**
Minimum 72 hours continuous observation for reliable pattern.
Record every observation with exact time.
Identify anomalies (deviations from pattern) – anomalies often indicate planning or security concern.
Compare observations across time: 0600 Tuesday vs 0600 Thursday.

**Analysis:**
Map activity timelines against observed patterns.
Identify predictable windows.
Identify gaps in pattern – times of vulnerability.
Identify deviation from pattern as indicator of change in plans or security posture.

**Limitations:**
Patterns can be false – deliberately established to create predictability (deception). Always consider possibility that pattern is a trap.
