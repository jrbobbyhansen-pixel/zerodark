# Water Procurement

## Finding Water Sources

Water is critical for survival. Priority sources:
1. **Moving water** — streams, rivers, flowing sources
2. **Still water** — springs, lakes, ponds
3. **Precipitation** — rain, snow, dew
4. **Underground** — dig in dry stream beds, green vegetation areas

## Purification Methods

### Boiling
- Boil for at least 1 minute (3 minutes at altitude)
- Kills bacteria, viruses, and most parasites
- Does NOT remove chemical contaminants

### Solar Still
- Dig hole, place container at bottom
- Cover with clear plastic, weight center with stone
- Sun heats ground, condensation runs down into container
- Produces ~1 liter per day in dry climates
- Removes impurities through evaporation/condensation

### Transpiration Bags
- Seal clear plastic bag around leafy green vegetation
- Place in sunlight 3-4 hours
- Water condenses on plastic interior
- Collect ~1 liter per day per bag
- Safe water; no boiling required

### Chemical Treatment
- Bleach: 8 drops per gallon, wait 30 minutes (half if heavily cloudy)
- Iodine tablets: follow package instructions
- Tablet form more stable than liquid for field use

### Filtering
- Cloth through multiple layers removes sediment
- Does NOT kill pathogens — must combine with boiling or chemical
- Sand/charcoal filters improve taste but need boiling afterward

## Water Discipline

Ration water to prevent dehydration. In hot environments:
- Drink small amounts frequently (avoid gulping)
- Avoid alcohol and tobacco (increase fluid loss)
- Rest during hottest hours
- Never drink urine or seawater

In cold climates, melt snow/ice with body heat or boiling — never eat it raw (risk of hypothermia).

## Signaling Water Sources

In arid terrain:
- Follow animal tracks (converge at water)
- Watch bird flight patterns (return to water at dawn/dusk)
- Green vegetation concentrates near water
- Insects and frogs indicate nearby water
