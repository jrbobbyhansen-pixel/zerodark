# Weather Reading and Prediction

## Cloud Types and Meanings

### Cumulus (Fair Weather)
- White, puffy, flat base
- Indicates fair weather if scattered
- If grows vertically into cumulonimbus, thunderstorms likely

### Stratus
- Gray, uniform layer
- Indicates overcast conditions
- Rain may follow if dark and low

### Cirrus
- Thin, wispy, high altitude (20,000+ feet)
- Indicates weather change in 24-48 hours
- Often precedes warm front

### Cumulonimbus (Thunderstorm)
- Towering, dark base, anvil top
- Severe weather: heavy rain, hail, lightning, strong winds
- Move to shelter immediately

## Pressure and Wind Indicators

### Rising Barometric Pressure
- Indicates improving weather
- Clear skies likely
- Wind becoming lighter

### Falling Barometric Pressure
- Indicates deteriorating weather
- Rain/storms likely within 12-24 hours
- Increase in wind speed

### Wind Direction Changes
- Wind shift from south to north: cold front approaching
- Consistent wind from one direction: stable weather
- Shift from west to east: unsettled weather

## Local Weather Indicators

### Animals and Insects
- Ants build higher mounds before heavy rain
- Spiders vacate webs before rain
- Birds fly low before storms
- Insects more active in warm, humid air masses

### Plant Indicators
- Leaves turn lighter underside: humidity rising, rain coming
- Pine cones open/close: responds to humidity
- Seaweed becomes damp: humidity increase signals moisture

### Natural Phenomena
- Rainbow in morning: rain likely that day (in temperate zones)
- Rainbow at evening: fair weather continues
- Halos around sun/moon: moisture in upper atmosphere, rain likely
- Red sky at night: fair weather continues; red sky morning: rain coming

## Reading Altitude Changes

- Rapid pressure drop: severe weather brewing
- Slow pressure change: gradual weather modification
- Temperature/humidity relationship: heat with high humidity = thunderstorm risk

## Seasonal Patterns

### Spring
- Frontal systems frequent; rapid weather changes
- Thunderstorm season begins
- Severe weather risk increases

### Summer
- Heat-driven thunderstorms in afternoon
- More stable air masses
- Less frontal activity in some regions

### Fall
- Rapid pressure changes more frequent
- Cold fronts bring abrupt temperature drops
- Hurricane/tropical system season (Atlantic)

### Winter
- Low pressure systems track across temperate latitudes
- Rapid temperature changes
- Precipitation more likely as snow in cold regions

## Survival Weather Considerations

### Before Weather Changes
- Secure shelter (winds will increase)
- Gather additional fuel, water
- Ensure clothing layers ready
- Check equipment for damage potential

### During Severe Weather
- Shelter from wind (reduces heat loss by 50%+)
- Stay dry (wet clothing conducts heat 25x faster)
- Avoid exposed heights (lightning risk)
- Keep moving in cold rain (prevent hypothermia)

### After Weather Event
- Assess shelter for damage
- Gather additional firewood (ground wet)
- Check water sources for contamination (flooding)
- Plan next movement based on new conditions
