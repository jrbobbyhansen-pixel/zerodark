# Food Procurement

## Universal Edibility Test

Use when plants are unidentified and nutritional need is high (2+ days without food).

**Procedure**:
1. Separate plant into parts (roots, stems, leaves, seeds)
2. Smell each part (reject if strong/rank odor)
3. Touch to skin, wait 15 minutes (reject if irritation)
4. Touch to lips, wait 3 minutes (reject if tingling/burning)
5. Place on tongue, wait 15 minutes (reject if taste test fails)
6. Swallow small amount, wait 5 hours (reject if any GI distress)
7. Eat larger amount if no distress

**Reject plants with**:
- Milky sap (except dandelion/fig)
- Umbrella-shaped seed heads (hemlock, water hemlock are deadly)
- Bean/pea pods without visible seeds
- Bulbs with papery covering (like onion skin)
- Three-leaf clusters (poison ivy, oak, sumac)

**Safe plants**:
- Dandelion (all parts edible)
- Cattails (roots, young shoots)
- Acorns (boil 5+ times to remove tannins)
- Pine nuts, seeds

## Hunting and Trapping

### Deadfall Trap
- Heavy log/rock balanced on two-stick trigger
- Baited with food
- Kills small game (rabbits, rodents)

### Snare
- Wire or paracord loop suspended on branch
- Animal walks through, loop tightens
- Most effective for rabbits, squirrels
- Check frequently (animal may escape or die)

### Fish Trap
- Funnel-shaped basket in stream current
- Fish enter but cannot exit
- Requires patience; needs daily checking

### Bow and Arrow (if materials available)
- Self-bow: flexible branch, sinew string
- Arrow: straight wood, stone/bone point
- Requires practice for accuracy

## Fishing

### Hand Line
- 20+ feet of cordage, hook (carved bone), bait
- Cast from shore, retrieve when felt pulling

### Weir (Fish Trap)
- Stone barrier in stream with gap
- Fish move upstream, concentrated at gap
- Net or spear fish at collection point

### Poison Fishing
- Crush leaves of plants containing saponins (soaproot, walnut)
- Scatter in still water
- Fish lose balance, rise to surface (collect before dead)
- Make water undrinkable downstream; use only in survival situations

## Insects and Edibles

**Edible insects**:
- Crickets, grasshoppers (remove legs/wings)
- Grubs, larvae (taste varies)
- Termites, ants (good protein)
- Locusts, cicadas

**Preparation**:
- Remove exoskeleton if large
- Roast over fire to kill parasites
- Sun-dry for storage

## Food Preservation

### Drying
- Sun-dry or smoke-dry meat/fish
- Requires 4-7 days depending on humidity
- Store in dry location

### Smoking
- Slow fire below meat rack
- Smoke kills surface bacteria
- Imparts preservative compounds

### Freezing
- In cold climates, freeze meat immediately
- Can last weeks/months if properly stored
- Thaw before eating (or eat frozen in extreme cold)

## Nutrition Priorities

In survival without food:
1. Carbohydrates (quick energy) — plants, nuts, seeds
2. Protein (muscle maintenance) — meat, insects, legumes
3. Fats (energy density) — nuts, animal fat, seeds

Average adult needs ~2000 calories/day; even partial calories extend survival.
