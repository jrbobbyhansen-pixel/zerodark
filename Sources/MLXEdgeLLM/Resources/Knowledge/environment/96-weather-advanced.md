# Advanced Field Weather – Prediction and Planning

**Pressure without instruments:** Smoke straight = stable high. Drifts down = low approaching. Spirals = unstable. Aches = pressure drop (unreliable alone). Fire tall straight = stable. Swirling = unstable.

**Cloud reading:** Cirrus (wispy) – fair now, weather 24–48hr. Increasing, lowering – deterioration 12–24hr. Cirrostratus (halo) – precipitation 24hr. Altocumulus (mackerel) – fair now, change 12–18hr. Cumulus (flat, vertical) – stable, building = afternoon storm possible. Cumulonimbus (anvil, dark) – immediate severe storm. Stratus (flat gray, low) – drizzle/rain, stable, poor visibility.

**Wind:** Veering (S–W–N) – improving. Backing (N–W–S) – deteriorating. Steady increase – approaching front. Gusty, variable – convective instability (thunderstorm risk).

**Dew point:** <4°F spread – fog/low clouds likely in hours.

**Red sky:** Night (dust/haze west) = high pressure = fair weather. Morning (haze east) = high moving east = low/weather from west.

**Lightning:** 5 sec flash-thunder = 1 mile. <30 sec = take cover. 30 min after last before resuming.
