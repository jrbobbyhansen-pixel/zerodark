# Maritime Survival

**Raft:** Board immediately. Right capsized: pull righting strap downwind. Sea anchor (drogue) immediately (reduce drift, prevent capsizing). Inventory equipment. Establish watches.

**Water:** NO seawater (osmotic dehydration accelerates death). Collect rain (canopy, surface). Solar still (plastic over tray in sun). Dew (night). Fish – squeeze flesh through cloth.

**Signaling:** Day – mirror, EPIRB, orange smoke. Night – strobe, whistle. Visible greater range than voice.

**Food:** Fish attracted to structure (raft gets fish in days). Improvised hook (any metal). Line (paracord strands). Flying fish, squid (night, light). Seabirds land (high protein).

**Exposure:** Stay out water (25x heat loss faster). Cover skin (UV burns fatal in days). Shade (any material). Ration movement (preserve water requirement).

**Seasickness:** Horizon focus. Avoid enclosed. Ginger (if available). Scopolamine patch. Dehydration risk – replace fluids aggressively.

**Marine life:** Shark – no urinate, no trailing extremities, no splashing. Strike eyes, gills, snout. Jellyfish – vinegar, don't rub. Stonefish, lionfish – deadly venomous, wear footwear.
