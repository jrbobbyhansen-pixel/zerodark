# Perimeter Defense

**Establishing perimeter:**
All-round defense at all times. No position left unoccupied. No gaps that can't be covered by fire. Interlocking fields of fire – adjacent positions can engage threat to each other's front.

**Sectors of fire:**
Assign each position a primary sector (direct front) and secondary sector (overlap with adjacent positions). Fire control: engage only within assigned sector unless directed otherwise.

**Sector sketch:**
Simple diagram of each position, terrain features, fields of fire, dead ground (areas that cannot be observed or engaged from position). Reviewed by leader for gaps.

**Listening posts/observation posts (LP/OP):**
Established 100–200m beyond perimeter. Provide early warning. Communications back to perimeter (wire, radio, visual signal). Rotate every 2 hours maximum – cold, stress, darkness degrade alertness.

**Tripwire early warning:**
Wire strung at ankle height with tin cans (stones inside), illumination flares, or noise makers. Establishes alert perimeter beyond fighting positions.

**Stand-to:**
All personnel at 100% alert 30 minutes before dawn and 30 minutes after dusk – historically highest-frequency attack times. No movement during stand-to.

**Challenges and passwords:**
Running password: challenge is randomly selected word, password is pre-established response. Changes daily minimum. Challenge from 5m – never allow unknown person closer before challenge is answered.
