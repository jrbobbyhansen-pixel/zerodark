# Camouflage and Concealment

**BLISS criteria:**
**B**lend – match background in color, texture, pattern
**L**ow silhouette – never skyline, keep profile low
**I**rregular shape – break up human body outline
**S**mall – minimize observable size
**S**ecluded – separated from obvious routes and locations

**Color:** Match dominant background. Forest: green/brown/black. Desert: tan/brown/gray. Arctic: white. Urban: gray/black/brown. Three-color pattern preferred – dominant base + secondary disruption + shadow dark.

**Shape disruption:** Human shape is immediately recognizable. Add vegetation, netting, irregular materials to break outline. Focus on head-shoulder silhouette (most recognizable from distance).

**Shine elimination:** All metal (buckles, weapons, optical lenses) taped, painted, or covered. Exposed skin (face, hands, neck) covered with paint or natural pigment. Glass eliminated unless operationally required.

**Natural vegetation:**
Use locally present species – imported vegetation creates visible anomaly.
Replace wilting vegetation every 2–4 hours.
Never cut vegetation immediately around position – creates obvious gap.
Weave into uniform distributed pattern, not clumped.

**Thermal camouflage:**
Reduce IR signature: space blanket under clothing reflects body heat, reduces thermal profile. Move during cooler periods when ambient temperature reduces contrast. Avoid hot surfaces (vehicle engine, fires) that create large thermal contrast.

**Movement:** Stillness is camouflage. Movement compromises all other camouflage. Freeze during observation periods.
