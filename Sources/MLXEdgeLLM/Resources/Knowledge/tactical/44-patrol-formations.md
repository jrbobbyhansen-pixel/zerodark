# Patrol Formations

**Wedge:**
Standard movement formation. Point man forward center, two flankers behind at 45° angles. Creates triangle. Provides all-round observation, flexible to transition to line or file. Standard spacing: 5m between personnel in woods, 20m in open.

**File:**
Single line. Used in restricted terrain (jungle, dense brush, narrow corridors). Most vulnerable to frontal and rear attack. Lead man takes point.

**Line:**
Personnel abreast, equal spacing. Maximum firepower to front. Vulnerable rear. Used for assault or movement toward known threat.

**Echelon (left/right):**
Modified wedge with all personnel stepped to one side. Used when threat is known to be on one flank. Provides mass firepower to that side.

**Patrol spacing:**
Close enough to maintain visual contact. Far enough that single burst/fragmentation doesn't hit multiple personnel. Woods: 5m. Open terrain: 20–35m. Never bunch – two grenades can't kill what one can't.

**Security halts:**
Every 15–30 minutes: hand signal, all stop. Listen. Observe. 360° security. Each person faces alternate directions. Hold 3–5 minutes. Continue only when satisfied.

**Point man duties:**
Navigate and maintain route. Detect threats early. Communicate contact (hand signals). Does NOT engage unless ambushed – point man breaks left or right, cover while rest of patrol reacts.
