# Forced Entry and Breaching

**Mechanical breaching:**
Halligan bar: fork end under door hinge or beside lock mechanism. Strike with mallet to drive gap. Lever to crack frame. Works on most residential and commercial doors.
Bolt cutters: place at junction of shackle and padlock body – not middle of shackle.
Battering ram: strike beside lock, at chest height – not door center. Lower third of door more structurally sound.

**Ballistic breaching:**
Shotgun breaching round or slug at lock mechanism. Do not use through door if occupants possible on other side. Clear path after – breach round fragments remain dangerous.

**Door analysis:**
Inward opening: force hinges OR lock side. Outward opening: force lock side only.
Wood frame: most vulnerable at lock (single point). Metal frame with concrete = much harder.
Double door: center deadbolt most vulnerable. Attack weakest point.

**Lock picking basics:**
Tension wrench: flat strip of metal (bobby pin folded) applying light rotational pressure to plug.
Pick: bent bobby pin tip lifting individual pins.
Apply very light tension (most common error = too much tension). Lift each pin until it sets (slight give). Rotate when all pins set.
Pin tumblers are most common, most pickable. Deadbolts: same technique, more pins.

**Vehicle:**
Rear quarter approach avoids engine block and driver sightlines. Slim jim between door and window frame, hook linkage. Rear window: rubber mallet around seal perimeter. Glass: strike corner with sharp object.
