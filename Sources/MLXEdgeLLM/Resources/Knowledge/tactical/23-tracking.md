# Tracking and Counter-Tracking

## Sign You Leave

- **Footprints**: depth = weight, stride = height/pace, toe drag = fatigue
- **Disturbed vegetation**: bent grass recovers in hours, broken stems take days
- **Transfer**: material from footwear transfers to surface, oils from skin transfer to objects
- **Scent**: fire smoke, food, soap, deodorant carries far

## Counter-Tracking

- Walk on hard surfaces, rocks, roots
- Step in streams (breaks scent trail)
- Vary pace and direction unpredictably
- Back-track and observe — if tracked, you'll see them on your trail
- Cat scratch: step onto rock, step off to side, back-track — creates false endpoint

## Reading Tracks

Establish stride length from one clear track. Identify age by soil dryness, insect activity in print, edge degradation. Project direction toward paths of least resistance (water, trails, high ground).
