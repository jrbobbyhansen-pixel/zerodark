# Defensive Positions – Construction

**Individual fighting position (foxhole):**
Minimum depth: armpit height when standing – this protects upper body while allowing engagement. Width: shoulder width + 12in. Length: body length.
Parapet: soil from excavation stacked in front and to sides – minimum 12in high, reinforced with sandbags if available. Provides additional protection.
Overhead cover: logs covered with soil, 18in deep – provides grenade and shrapnel protection.
Field of fire: clear vegetation creating engagement zone to front and flanks.

**Two-man fighting position:**
L-shaped. Both positions provide mutual cover – if one position is flanked, the other engages the flanking element.

**Three priorities:**
1. Fields of fire (engage enemy before they engage you – clear vegetation)
2. Cover from fire (protection from direct fire – thick earth, logs, concrete, vehicles)
3. Concealment (prevents observation – vegetation, camouflage)
Cover and concealment are different – concealment does not protect from bullets.

**Hasty position:**
No time to dig – use natural terrain. Tree root ball, rock, ditch, vehicle. Improves as time permits. Anything is better than nothing.

**Deliberate position:**
Time available. Dig minimum 18in. Reinforce with sandbags, logs. Overhead cover priority. Interlocking fields of fire with adjacent positions.
