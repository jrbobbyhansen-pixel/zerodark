# Setting Up an Observation Post (OP)

## OCOKA Terrain Analysis

- **O**bservation and fields of fire
- **C**over and concealment
- **O**bstacles (natural and man-made)
- **K**ey terrain (ground giving decisive advantage)
- **A**venues of approach (routes enemy can use)

## OP Site Criteria

- Dominant ground with observation over objective
- Cover from direct fire (rocks, walls, terrain masking)
- Concealment from observation (vegetation, shadows, terrain)
- Two covered withdrawal routes minimum
- 360° observation capability when possible

## Construction

Natural materials only. No cut vegetation from immediate area (creates visible gaps). Improve existing vegetation. Overhead cover prevents observation from above. Minimize all site disturbance.

## Occupation

Approach from non-obvious route (never straight line to OP). Sanitize route (no tracks, no disturbed vegetation). Arrive before expected activity window.

## SALUTE Report

**Size / Activity / Location / Unit / Time / Equipment**

Record everything with exact times.
