# Stealth Movement and Noise Discipline

**Sound environment:**
Twig snap: 100m in still air. Voices: 300m in open terrain. Metal clank: 200m. Consistent noise (footsteps): 50–100m. Be aware of sound travel in different terrain – water amplifies, forest dampens.

**Foot placement:**
Heel to toe, transferring weight slowly. Test each step before committing weight. Avoid dry leaves, twigs, gravel where possible. Move through – not on – dry material. Still water preferable to grass for sound.

**Gear discipline:**
All metal taped or padded (buckles, carabiners, dog tags). Nothing loose to rattle (tape all). Wool and fleece preferred over nylon (nylon swishing audible). Remove all keys, coins, anything that moves. Secure all straps.

**Body movement:**
Breathing: slow, deep, through nose. Reduces audible breathing and allows better listening. Short freezes every 5 minutes – listen actively, let ears adapt. Control cough/sneeze: press firmly on upper lip.

**Movement techniques:**
High crawl (hands/knees): faster, higher profile. Low crawl (elbows/knees): slowest, lowest signature. Rush (3–5 seconds): short unpredictable sprint between cover. Freeze on any sound.

**Night:**
Initial dark adaptation: 20 minutes. Maximum: 45 minutes. Peripheral vision superior at night (more sensitive rods). Scan by looking 10–15° to the side of object. Use cover of wind noise for movement.

**Realistic rates:**
Dense jungle: 200m/hour (stealthy). Woodland: 500m/hour. Open terrain night: 1km/hour.
