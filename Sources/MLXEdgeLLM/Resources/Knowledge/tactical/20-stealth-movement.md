# Stealth Movement and Noise Discipline

## Sound Travel

Snapping twig carries 100m+ in still air. Voices carry 300m+ in open terrain.

## Foot Placement

Roll foot heel to toe. Test weight before committing to each step. Freeze 5 seconds after any noise you make.

## Gear Preparation

Tape all metal (buckles, clips). Secure all loose items. Use wool/fleece instead of nylon (quieter). Remove keys and coins. Avoid synthetic fabrics that swish.

## Movement Techniques

### High Crawl
Hands and knees, faster but more visible.

### Low Crawl
Elbows and knees, belly near ground, slowest but lowest profile.

### Rush
3-5 second unpredictable bursts between cover.

## Night Adaptation

20 minutes for initial dark adaptation. Maximum at 45 minutes. Use peripheral vision (rods more sensitive, distributed around retina periphery).

## Wind Use

Move into or across wind so scent blows away from dogs and personnel.

## Realistic Rate

Stealth patrol 1km/hour in unknown terrain is realistic.
