# Tracking and Counter-Tracking

**Sign categories:**
Ground sign: footprints, crushed grass, displaced rocks, scratches.
Transfer: material from foot sole transferred to surface, oils from skin on objects.
Disturbance: bent vegetation, disturbed insect activity, displaced objects.
Scent: bodily odor, equipment smell, food/tobacco.

**Footprint analysis:**
Depth = relative weight of individual. Stride length correlates to height and pace. Toe drag = fatigue or injury. Erratic pattern = injured or dark/unfamiliar terrain. Spacing change = pace change or terrain change.

**Track age:**
Fresh: sharp, crisp edges, moist soil. Hours: edges beginning to dry, insects beginning to fill print. Day: crust formation, print filling, vegetation recovering. Check: push finger beside print to same depth – compare edge freshness to existing print.

**Counter-tracking techniques:**
Walk on rocks, hard surfaces, fallen logs. Step in streams (breaks scent trail and leaves no print). Walk backwards for 20m then resume forward. Cat scratch: step onto boulder, step off to side, backtrack – creates false endpoint. Change direction at hard surface. Vary pace unpredictably.

**Tracker detection:**
If you are being tracked: they must be behind you on your trail. Find elevated observation point. Wait with observation back on your trail. Movement toward you before you've moved = tracker.
