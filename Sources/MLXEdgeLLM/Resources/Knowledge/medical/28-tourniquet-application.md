# Tourniquet Application – Detailed Procedure

**Commercial (CAT, SOFTT-W):**
1. Apply 2–3 inches above wound (not over joint)
2. Route through buckle and pull tight
3. Tighten windlass until bleeding stops
4. Lock windlass in clip
5. Write time on white strap or patient's forehead

**Self-application (one-handed):**
Bite free end of tourniquet. Thread over limb and through buckle. Pull tight with teeth and one hand. Tighten windlass – may take significant effort with one hand.

**Improvised tourniquet:**
Fold triangular bandage, belt, or other material into 2–3in wide band. Wrap around limb 2in above wound. Tie half-knot, place stick on knot, tie full knot over stick. Twist stick until bleeding stops. Secure stick to prevent unwinding with additional wrap.

**When to use:**
Amputation. Arterial bleeding not controlled by direct pressure. Wound location where direct pressure cannot be maintained. Bleeding from explosion, crush injury, gunshot wound to extremity.

**Critical don'ts:**
Do not apply over clothing (cannot assess tightness). Do not apply over joint (ineffective). Do not release in field (clot disruption causes renewed hemorrhage). Do not assume it's tight enough if patient still in significant pain from wound (pain is expected from the tourniquet itself).

**Conversion:**
If time allows and resources permit: wound pack – pressure dressing – carefully remove tourniquet while maintaining pressure. This is a clinical decision – only in controlled environment with ability to replace if bleeding resumes.
