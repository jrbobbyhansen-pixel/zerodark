# TCCC – Tactical Combat Casualty Care

Standard for all combat and high-risk tactical environments.

**Three phases:**
Care Under Fire: Return fire. Self-aid only if possible without exposing self. Tourniquet own wound if needed. Drag casualty to cover using rifle sling or drag handles.
Tactical Field Care: Behind cover or concealment. Systematic assessment. More complete treatment.
CASEVAC: Preparation for movement to higher level of care.

**MARCH Protocol (in order):**
**M – Massive hemorrhage:** Apply tourniquet, wound pack, pressure dressing. High and tight.
**A – Airway:** Open and maintain. NPA for unconscious.
**R – Respiration:** Assess breathing. Tension pneumothorax, sucking chest wounds.
**C – Circulation:** Additional bleeding sites. IV access if trained.
**H – Hypothermia:** Casualty blanket IMMEDIATELY after life threats managed.

**Tension pneumothorax:**
Signs: absent breath sounds one side, tracheal deviation away, distended neck veins, shock, respiratory distress.
Treatment: 14-gauge needle (or largest available), 2nd intercostal space, midclavicular line OR 4th–5th ICS anterior axillary line. Rush of air = success. May need to repeat.

**Sucking chest wound:**
Occlusive dressing taped 3 sides (vented). Monitor for tension pneumo. If deteriorates after sealing, burp or remove seal.

**Blood sweep:**
After initial treatment: roll patient forward, check entire back for hidden injuries.
