# Fractures and Dislocations

Signs: Deformity, abnormal movement, crepitus (grating sound), severe point tenderness, swelling, bruising, inability to bear weight.

## Splinting Rule

Immobilize joint ABOVE and BELOW fracture. Pad all bony prominences. Check CSM (circulation/sensation/movement) before and after.

## Improvised Splints

Straight sticks, trekking poles, rolled sleeping pad, magazines. Bind firmly – not tight enough to restrict circulation.

## Open Fracture (Bone Visible)

Cover with clean cloth. Do not push bone back. Irrigate wound. Evacuate immediately.

## Femur Fracture

Can lose 1-2 liters of blood into thigh. Treat aggressively for shock.

## Spine

Any unconscious trauma patient – assume spinal injury. Logroll maintaining head-neck-spine alignment.
