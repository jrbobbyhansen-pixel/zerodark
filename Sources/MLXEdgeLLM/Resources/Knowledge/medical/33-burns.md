# Burns – Assessment and Treatment

**Rule of nines (adult body surface area):**
Head/neck: 9%. Each arm: 9%. Chest: 9%. Abdomen: 9%. Upper back: 9%. Lower back: 9%. Each thigh: 9%. Each lower leg: 9%. Genitals: 1%.

**Severity:**
First degree: Red, dry, painful. Sunburn equivalent. Treat with cooling.
Second degree: Blisters, moist, very painful. Treat as wound – cover, do not pop (sterile barrier).
Third degree: Leathery, white or charred, painless (nerve destruction). Serious. Cover, evacuate.

**Treatment:**
Cooling: cool (NOT cold) running water for 20 full minutes within 3 hours of burn. Cold causes vasoconstriction and worsens damage. Never use ice.
Do NOT: Butter, toothpaste, oil, any home remedy. These trap heat and cause infection.
Dressings: Non-stick sterile. Loosen frequently to check circulation. Rewrap daily.

**Critical burns requiring immediate evacuation:**
Burns >20% BSA (both arms, or both legs, or chest and abdomen)
Any third-degree burn
Burns to face, hands, feet, genitals, or joints
Inhalation burns: singed facial hair, hoarse voice, soot in nostrils, carbonaceous sputum – airways can swell shut in hours

**Fluid replacement:** Burns >15% BSA cause severe fluid loss. Oral rehydration salts. IV if available and trained.

**Chemical burns:** Brush off dry chemical first. Flush with copious water 20+ minutes. Do not attempt to neutralize acid with base or vice versa.
