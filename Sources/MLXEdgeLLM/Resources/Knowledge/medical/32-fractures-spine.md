# Fractures, Dislocations, and Spinal Injuries

**Fracture signs:** Deformity, abnormal movement, crepitus (bone grinding), point tenderness, swelling, bruising, inability to bear weight or use limb.

**Splinting principle:** Immobilize the joint ABOVE and joint BELOW the fracture. Pad all bony prominences. Check circulation/sensation/movement (CSM) before AND after every splint.

**Improvised splints:** Straight sticks, trekking poles, sleeping pad sections, magazines, cardboard. All need padding between skin and rigid material.

**Binding:** Firm but not tight enough to restrict circulation. Test: insert finger between bind and skin – should fit but be snug. Recheck every 30 minutes.

**Open fracture:** Bone visible through skin. Cover with cleanest available dressing. Do not push bone back. Irrigate wound around bone. Splint. Evacuate – high infection risk.

**Femur fracture:** Life-threatening. Can bleed 1–2 liters into thigh (entire blood volume is 5L). Signs: pain, deformity, swelling of thigh, shock. Traction splint if available – reduces bleeding and pain. Treat aggressively for shock.

**Dislocation:** Do not attempt reduction unless specifically trained in that joint. Exception: remote wilderness with no evacuation available + trained person. Splint as found. Ice.

**Spinal injury precaution:** Any unconscious trauma patient – treat as spinal until proven otherwise. Logroll: maintain head-neck-spine alignment throughout. One person stabilizes head, second rolls. Do not hyperextend neck.
