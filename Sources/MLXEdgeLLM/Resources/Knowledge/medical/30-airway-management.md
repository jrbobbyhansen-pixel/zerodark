# Airway Management

**Open airway – unconscious patient:**
Head-tilt chin-lift: one hand on forehead, push back. Other hand under chin, lift. This moves tongue off posterior pharynx.
Jaw thrust (suspected spinal injury): hands on sides of jaw, push mandible forward. Do not move neck.

**Look Listen Feel:**
Look: chest rise and fall. Listen: air movement at mouth and nose. Feel: airflow on cheek. Do this for 10 seconds before declaring no breathing.

**Recovery position:**
Roll patient onto side (toward you). Top knee pulled forward to prevent rolling back. Top hand under cheek. Monitors airway, prevents aspiration of vomit.

**Choking – conscious adult:**
5 firm back blows between shoulder blades with heel of hand. 5 abdominal thrusts (fist above navel, thumb side in, sharp inward-upward thrust). Alternate until cleared or unconscious. If unconscious: start CPR.

**Choking – unconscious:**
Lower to ground carefully. Start CPR. Check mouth before each breath cycle – remove object if visible.

**CPR:**
Heel of hand on center of chest (lower half of sternum). 2in depth. 100 beats per minute. 30 compressions + 2 breaths. Hands-only acceptable if untrained or unwilling to give breaths. Do not stop unless patient resumes breathing, help takes over, or physically exhausted.

**Nasopharyngeal airway (NPA):**
Lubricate. Insert with bevel toward nasal septum. Advance until flange rests at nostril. Do not force. Use in unconscious breathing patient to maintain airway.
