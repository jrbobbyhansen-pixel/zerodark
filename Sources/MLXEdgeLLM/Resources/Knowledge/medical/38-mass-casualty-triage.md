# Mass Casualty Triage – START Protocol

When casualties outnumber available providers, triage determines order of care.

**START (Simple Triage and Rapid Treatment) – 30-second assessment:**

**Step 1:** Order all who can walk to move to designated area – TAG GREEN (minor)

**Step 2:** Remaining casualties – assess in order:

**Respirations:**
None – reposition airway – still none – TAG BLACK (expectant/deceased)
>30 per minute – TAG RED (immediate)
<30 per minute – continue to step 3

**Perfusion:**
Radial pulse absent OR capillary refill >2 seconds – TAG RED (immediate)
Radial pulse present AND capillary refill <2 seconds – continue to step 4

**Mental status:**
Cannot follow simple commands – TAG RED (immediate)
Can follow simple commands – TAG YELLOW (delayed)

**Tags:**
BLACK: Deceased or unsurvivable injury – no resources expended
RED: Immediate – life-threatening, survivable with immediate intervention
YELLOW: Delayed – serious but stable, can wait 1+ hours
GREEN: Minor – walking wounded, can wait or self-treat

**Do during initial triage:** Control life-threatening bleeding only. Open airway (reposition only). Nothing else.

**Reassess:** Triage status changes. Reassess all patients every 10–15 minutes.
