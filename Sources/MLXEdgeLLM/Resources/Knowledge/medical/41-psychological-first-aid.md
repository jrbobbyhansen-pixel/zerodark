# Psychological First Aid and Combat Stress

**Combat/trauma stress reactions (NORMAL responses to abnormal events):**
Shaking, tearfulness, anger, withdrawal, hypervigilance, intrusive thoughts, sleep disruption, appetite loss. These are normal in the immediate aftermath.

**Psychological first aid (STOP):**
**S**afety: Move to safe area. Physical safety is psychological safety first.
**T**alk: Brief, calm, non-probing conversation. "I'm here with you." Do not ask them to relive the event.
**O**rientation: Help them understand what happened and what happens next. Predictability reduces panic.
**P**ractical: Address immediate physical needs – water, warmth, food, communication.

**Acute stress reaction – intervention:**
Breathing: slow deep breaths together. Breathe in 4 counts, hold 4, out 4. Physiologically reduces anxiety.
Grounding: name 5 things you can see, 4 you can touch, 3 you can hear, 2 you can smell, 1 you can taste.
Physical: remove from trigger environment if possible. Movement (walking) dissipates stress hormones.

**Combat stress indicators requiring removal:**
Inability to follow orders. Uncontrollable shaking. Paralysis (freezing). Harmful to self or others. Dissociation (not recognizing reality).

**Long-term PTSD prevention:**
Early debriefing within 24–48 hours in small groups. Do not isolate. Restore sense of control. Peer support > professional counseling in acute phase.

**Leader responsibility:** Monitor all personnel. Normalize stress reactions. Identify those needing escalation.
