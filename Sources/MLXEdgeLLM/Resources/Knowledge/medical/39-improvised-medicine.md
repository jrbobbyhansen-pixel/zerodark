# Improvised Medical Resources

**Improvised wound irrigation:**
Plastic bag with pinhole or syringe. 300–600mL clean water at pressure. Removes contamination better than dabbing or pouring.

**Improvised suture:**
20lb+ fishing line, dental floss, fine braided thread. Simple interrupted pattern: 4–5mm apart, 4mm from edge. Surgeon's knot (double first throw). Leave 3mm tails. Remove in 7–10 days (face 5 days, joints 14 days).

**Natural antimicrobials:**
Honey (raw): directly into wound, occlusive dressing. Strong antimicrobial, promotes healing, reduces infection. Widely used in wound care. Manuka honey most potent but any raw honey has effect.
Garlic: crush, extract juice, dilute 1:3 with clean water for wound wash. Active compound allicin has antimicrobial properties.
Propolis: tree resin collected by bees, potent antimicrobial.

**Pain management:**
Willow bark tea: strip bark from willow (Salix species), steep in boiling water 20 minutes. Contains salicylates (aspirin precursor). Effective for mild to moderate pain.
Positioning: elevate injured extremity above heart. Reduces pain 30–50%.
Cold: reduces pain and swelling. Cool (not ice-cold) water or cool cloth.

**Improvised saline:**
1/4 tsp non-iodized salt per liter boiled water. Use for wound irrigation only, not IV.

**Splinting improvised:**
Any rigid material padded with cloth. Check CSM every 30 minutes. Better slightly loose than too tight.
