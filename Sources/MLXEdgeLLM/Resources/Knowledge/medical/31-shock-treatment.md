# Shock – Recognition and Treatment

**Definition:** Inadequate perfusion of tissues. Cells receive insufficient oxygen. Rapidly fatal if untreated.

**Types:**
- **Hypovolemic** (most common in trauma): blood/fluid loss
- **Distributive** (septic, anaphylactic): blood vessel dilation
- **Obstructive** (tension pneumothorax, cardiac tamponade): obstruction to flow
- **Cardiogenic**: pump failure

**Signs and progression:**
Early (compensated): Anxiety, rapid heart rate (>100), pale/cool skin, thirst, slight confusion
Late (decompensated): BP drops, confusion worsens, skin mottled, breathing very rapid
Irreversible: Organ failure, unresponsive, imminent death

**Treatment:**
1. Stop the source (control bleeding – first priority)
2. Position: supine, legs elevated 12in (UNLESS head/chest injury or breathing difficulty)
3. Insulate: casualty blanket – hypothermia accelerates shock death
4. Airway: ensure open
5. Calm reassurance
6. Oral fluids: if conscious, no abdominal injury, no expected surgery – small sips

**Anaphylactic shock:**
Epinephrine 0.3mg IM outer thigh (EpiPen) immediately. Repeat in 5–15 minutes if no improvement. Antihistamine secondary. Lay flat, legs up. Oxygen if available.

**Signs of improvement:** Pulse slows and strengthens. Skin warms and color returns. Mental clarity improves. Urine output resumes (if monitored).
