# Heat Injuries – Recognition and Treatment

**Heat cramps:** Painful muscle spasms during exertion in heat. Cause: sodium depletion from sweating. Treatment: rest in cool area, fluids with electrolytes (salt), stretch affected muscles. Return to activity when resolved.

**Heat exhaustion:** Heavy sweating, weakness, pale cool clammy skin, nausea, headache, fainting. Core temp <104°F. Cause: fluid and electrolyte depletion. Treatment: remove from heat immediately, cool area, loosen clothing, cool wet cloths to skin, fluids with electrolytes if conscious. Rest 24 hours minimum.

**Heat stroke (EMERGENCY):**
Classic: elderly, in hot environment, NO sweating, hot DRY skin, confusion, altered mental status. Core temp >104°F.
Exertional: athletes/soldiers, MAY still sweat, confusion, altered mental status.
Both = immediate life threat.

**Heat stroke treatment:**
Cool immediately by any available means – this takes priority over everything else.
Cold water immersion if available (most effective): ice bath, lake, pool.
Evaporative cooling: cool water misted or applied, fan aggressively.
Target: drop 1°F per 5 minutes. Stop cooling at 102°F to prevent overshoot to hypothermia.
Cool neck, armpits, groin (major blood vessels close to surface).
Evacuate immediately after cooling begins.

**Prevention:**
Acclimatization: 1–2 weeks of graded exposure reduces heat injury risk 90%.
Hydration: urine should be pale yellow. Clear = overhydrated (hyponatremia risk).
Activity modification: heavy exertion during coolest hours (before 1000, after 1600).
Buddy system: recognize heat injury in others before they recognize it in themselves.
