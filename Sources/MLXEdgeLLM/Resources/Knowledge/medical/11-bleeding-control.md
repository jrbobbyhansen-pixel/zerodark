# Bleeding Control – Hemorrhage is the #1 Preventable Cause of Death

Arterial bleeding kills in under 3 minutes. Venous bleeding: minutes to hours.

## Direct Pressure

Press hard with cloth. Do NOT lift to check – disrupts clot formation. Add more material over saturated cloth. Maintain minimum 10 minutes continuous firm pressure.

## Tourniquet (Limbs Only, NOT Neck/Groin/Axilla)

2-3 inches above wound. Tighten until bright red bleeding stops completely. Pain is expected and necessary. Note exact time applied. Mark on patient's skin with marker or blood. DO NOT remove in field.

## Improvised Tourniquet

Minimum 2in wide (NOT wire, cord, shoelaces – cause tissue damage). Windlass stick twisted until bleeding stops. Secure windlass against skin.

## Wound Packing (Junctional Wounds – Groin/Axilla/Neck)

Pack gauze directly into cavity. Maintain firm constant pressure minimum 3 minutes.

## Signs of Arterial Bleeding

Bright red, pulsing, spurting – life-threatening immediately.
