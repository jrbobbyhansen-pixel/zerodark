# Hypothermia Treatment

## Core Temperature Assessment

**Normal**: 37°C (98.6°F)
**Mild Hypothermia**: 32-35°C (90-95°F)
**Moderate**: 28-32°C (82-90°F)
**Severe**: Below 28°C (Below 82°F)

## Mild Hypothermia Treatment

### Signs
- Shivering (violent)
- Confusion, poor coordination
- Slurred speech
- Irrational decisions ("paradoxical undressing")

### Field Treatment
1. Move to shelter, remove wet clothing
2. Wrap in dry insulation (sleeping bag, blankets)
3. Give warm drinks (no alcohol, caffeine)
4. Apply external heat: fire, body-to-body contact
5. Monitor closely (may progress to moderate)
6. Allow gradual rewarming

## Moderate Hypothermia Treatment

### Signs
- Shivering stopped (danger sign)
- Severe confusion, drowsiness
- Loss of fine motor control
- Slurred speech continues
- May refuse help

### Field Treatment
1. Move to shelter carefully (avoid jostling)
2. Remove wet clothing
3. Insulate from ground heavily
4. Passive rewarming (body heat, blankets)
5. Monitor airway (may become unconscious)
6. Do NOT apply direct heat (risk of afterdrop)
7. Consider evacuation (medical care appropriate)

## Severe Hypothermia Treatment

### Signs (May Appear Dead)
- Unconscious
- Minimal pulse/breathing (barely detectable)
- Muscle rigidity
- Fixed, dilated pupils
- Appears lifeless

### Field Treatment
1. Treat gently (avoid sudden movement)
2. Check pulse for 30-60 seconds
3. If no pulse but appears salvageable, begin CPR
4. Move to shelter very carefully
5. Passive external rewarming only
6. Do NOT rewarm aggressively (afterdrop risk)
7. Evacuation critical (extracorporeal rewarming may be possible)

## Active vs. Passive Rewarming

### Passive Rewarming (Field Standard)
- Allow body to generate own heat
- Insulate from cold
- Wrap in blankets, sleeping bag
- Internal heat sources: warm drinks
- Safe, doesn't cause complications

### Active Core Rewarming (Medical Facility)
- IV heated fluids
- Heated oxygen (if intubated)
- Extracorporeal rewarming (heart-lung bypass)
- For severe hypothermia

### Active External Rewarming (AVOID Field)
- Risk of afterdrop (paradoxical temperature drop)
- Heat applied externally redirects blood to skin
- Core temperature drops further
- Dangerous in field setting

## Afterdrop Prevention

**What is Afterdrop**:
- Paradoxical drop in core temperature after rewarming begins
- Caused by peripheral vasodilation
- Can be fatal if unrecognized

**Prevention**:
- Avoid aggressive external rewarming
- Use passive rewarming
- Avoid sudden heat application
- Reposition patient minimally

## Specific Management

### Conscious Patient (Mild-Moderate)
- Oral rewarming: warm (not hot) drinks
- Sugars: body needs fuel for heat production
- Avoid alcohol, caffeine (increase heat loss)

### Unconscious Patient
- Do NOT give oral fluids (aspiration risk)
- Passive rewarming only
- Monitor airway
- Position for drainage

### Rewarming Duration
- Mild: hours to days
- Moderate: days
- Severe: may require extracorporeal rewarming

## Medication Considerations

**During Rewarming**:
- Continue CPR despite long duration
- "Nobody is dead until warm and dead"
- Some severe hypothermia patients survive

## Complications During Rewarming

### Afterdrop (Discussed Above)
### Arrhythmias
- Hypothermic heart sensitive to movement
- May go into VF with jostling
- Gentle handling critical

### Pulmonary Edema
- Fluid accumulation in lungs
- May develop during rewarming
- Monitor breathing

## Prevention

- Avoid getting wet
- Layer clothing properly
- Know wind chill effects
- Recognize early signs
- Seek shelter early
- Maintain nutrition and hydration

## Wind Chill Effects

At 20°F (-7°C) with wind:
- 10 mph wind: feels like 9°F (-13°C)
- 20 mph wind: feels like 4°F (-16°C)
- 40 mph wind: feels like -17°F (-27°C)

Hypothermia risk increases dramatically with wind.

## Special Populations

### Elderly
- May not shiver effectively
- Hypothermia develops insidiously
- More susceptible to complications

### Infants/Children
- Lose heat faster than adults
- Rewarming may take longer
- Monitor closely

### Alcohol/Drug Users
- Impaired heat production
- Poor decision-making (don't recognize danger)
- Higher mortality
