# Bleeding Control – Life-Threatening Hemorrhage

Arterial hemorrhage kills in under 3 minutes. Massive hemorrhage is the #1 preventable cause of death in trauma.

**Identify:** Bright red, pulsing, spurting = arterial – immediate life threat. Dark red, steady flow = venous – minutes to hours. Oozing = capillary – not immediately life-threatening.

**Direct pressure:**
Firm pressure with cleanest available material. Do NOT lift to check – disrupts clot. Add material on top of saturated cloth (removing disrupts clot). Continuous minimum 10 minutes. Use body weight, not just hand strength.

**Tourniquet (limbs only – NOT effective on neck/groin/axilla):**
Placement: 2–3 inches above wound, not over joint. Tighten until bright red bleeding stops completely. Pain is expected – adequate tightness is the goal. Time must be documented. Do NOT remove in field even if bleeding appears stopped. Maximum tourniquet time: 2 hours before tissue damage risk, but survival trumps limb salvage.

**Improvised tourniquet:**
Minimum 2in wide (cord, wire, shoelaces cause tissue damage and are inadequate). Belt can work if 2in wide. Windlass: stick twisted until bleeding stops. Secure windlass against skin to prevent unwinding.

**Junctional wound packing (groin, axilla, neck):**
Tourniquet ineffective here. Pack wound with gauze deep into cavity – do not drape over surface. Apply with fingers, packing tightly. Maintain constant firm pressure minimum 3 minutes. Do not release.

**Wound packing technique:**
Use entire gauze, packing deeply. After removal, repack if still bleeding. Apply pressure dressing over packed wound.
