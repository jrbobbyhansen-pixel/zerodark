# Tactical Combat Casualty Care (TCCC)

Three phases: Care Under Fire (return fire, apply tourniquet one-handed if needed, drag to cover) – Tactical Field Care (behind cover, systematic assessment) – CASEVAC.

## MARCH Protocol

- **M**assive hemorrhage: tourniquets, wound packing
- **A**irway: open and maintain
- **R**espiration: breathing, chest injuries
- **C**irculation: IV access if trained, remaining bleeding
- **H**ypothermia: casualty blanket immediately

## Needle Decompression (Tension Pneumothorax)

Signs: absent breath sounds one side + tracheal deviation + shock. 14-gauge needle at 2nd intercostal space, midclavicular line. Rush of air = success.

## Chest Seal

Sucking chest wound: seal with occlusive dressing (Vaseline gauze, plastic wrap), tape 3 sides (leaves vent for pressure relief). Monitor for tension pneumo.

## Blood Sweep

Roll casualty to check for hidden bleeding after primary assessment.
