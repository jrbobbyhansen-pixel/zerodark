# 9-Line MEDEVAC Request

Standard format for requesting medical evacuation. Know this cold – you may be calling for yourself or your teammate.

**Line 1:** Location of pickup site (10-digit MGRS grid)
**Line 2:** Radio frequency and call sign of requesting unit
**Line 3:** Number of patients by precedence
- A (Urgent): life-threatening within 2 hours
- B (Urgent Surgical): surgical intervention required within 2 hours
- C (Priority): serious but can wait up to 4 hours
- D (Routine): stable, evacuation within 24 hours
**Line 4:** Special equipment required (hoist, jungle penetrator, extraction equipment)
**Line 5:** Number of patients by type (litter vs ambulatory)
**Line 6:** Security at pickup site (N=no enemy troops, P=possible enemy, E=enemy troops nearby, X=armed escort required)
**Line 7:** Method of marking landing zone (smoke, panels, lights, none)
**Line 8:** Patient nationality and status (military/civilian, US/ally/enemy POW)
**Line 9:** NBC contamination (nuclear/biological/chemical)

**Example:**
"MEDEVAC, MEDEVAC, MEDEVAC. This is [call sign] with a 9-line. Line 1: 14SNA06450285. Line 2: [frequency] [call sign]. Line 3: 1 Alpha, 1 Delta. Line 4: None. Line 5: 1 litter, 1 ambulatory. Line 6: November. Line 7: Smoke. Line 8: 2 US military. Line 9: None. Break. Repeat: [repeat from line 1]."
