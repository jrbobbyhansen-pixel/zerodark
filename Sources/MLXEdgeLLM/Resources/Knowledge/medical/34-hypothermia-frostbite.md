# Hypothermia and Frostbite

**Hypothermia stages:**
Mild (95–98°F/35–36.7°C): Shivering, cold skin, impaired coordination. Treat: add insulation, warm environment.
Moderate (86–95°F/30–35°C): Shivering stops (ominous), confusion, stumbling, drowsiness.
Severe (<86°F/<30°C): Unconscious, cardiac risk, appears dead. Do not assume death – "not dead until warm and dead."

**Treatment:**
1. Remove wet clothing (wet conducts heat away 25× faster than dry)
2. Insulate from ground
3. Add insulation layers around body
4. Warm core first (NOT extremities – peripheral warming before core warming causes cold blood rush = cardiac arrest risk). Apply warmth to neck, armpits, groin.
5. Warm sweet fluids if conscious and swallowing normally
6. Do not rub extremities
7. Handle gently – cardiac arrhythmia risk

**Frostbite:**
White, gray, waxy, hard tissue. Deep frostbite: woody texture, no sensation.
Do NOT rewarm if risk of refreezing – refreeze-thaw cycle is catastrophic.
Rewarm in 100–104°F water 15–30 minutes. Extremely painful (pain = nerves recovering = good sign).
Do NOT rub. Do not break blisters. Keep elevated. Evacuate.

**Frostnip:** Redness, pain, but tissue still pliable. Rewarm with body heat. Does not require medical care.

**Wind chill:** 32°F + 20mph = 20°F effective. 0°F + 30mph = -26°F effective. Exposed skin freezes in 30 minutes at -26°F effective.
