# Wound Infection and Sepsis

**Normal wound healing timeline:**
Day 1–2: Redness, swelling, warmth – normal inflammatory response, NOT infection
Day 3–5: If resolving, healing is normal. If increasing, suspect infection.
Day 5–14: Wound should be closing, redness resolving, pain decreasing

**Infection signs:**
Early: Increasing (not decreasing) redness beyond wound edges. Increasing warmth. Increasing swelling. Cloudy discharge.
Late: Purulent (pus) discharge. Foul odor. Red streaks extending from wound (lymphangitis – emergency). Swollen tender lymph nodes in area. Fever.

**Sepsis:** Infection that has spread to bloodstream. Life-threatening.
Signs: Fever (>38.3°C/101°F) OR hypothermia (<36°C/96.8°F). Rapid heart rate. Rapid breathing. Confusion. Decreased urine. Skin may become mottled.
Treatment: Antibiotics, IV fluids. This is a hospital condition – trigger evacuation.

**Antibiotics if available:**
Amoxicillin/clavulanate: broad-spectrum, good for wound infections, bites
Ciprofloxacin: gram-negative coverage, UTI, GI infections, anthrax post-exposure
Doxycycline: tick-borne illnesses, respiratory infections, broad spectrum
Metronidazole: anaerobic bacteria, gut infections, abscesses
Take full course. Never half-dose.

**Maggot debridement:**
Maggots in wound eat necrotic tissue only – actually helpful for debridement. Gently remove with tweezers when dead or during dressing changes. Cover wound loosely to allow airflow.
