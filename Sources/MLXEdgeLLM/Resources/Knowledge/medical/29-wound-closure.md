# Wound Closure and Wound Care

**Wound assessment:**
Size, depth, location, contamination level, time since injury. Longer than 6 hours = contaminated, leave open. Animal bites = leave open (infection risk). Puncture wounds = leave open. Clean lacerations within 6 hours = can close.

**Wound irrigation:**
Most important step. 300–600mL clean or sterile water under pressure (pierced bag, syringe, improvised). Remove all visible debris. Irrigate until visibly clean. Pouring without pressure is inadequate.

**Closure methods:**
- Steri-strips or butterfly bandages: bring wound edges together, apply strips perpendicular to wound
- Improvised butterfly: cut medical tape or duct tape into butterfly shape
- Sutures (improvised): 20lb+ fishing line, dental floss, fine braided thread. Simple interrupted pattern. 4–5mm apart, 4mm from wound edge. Surgeon's knot (throw extra half-hitch first loop).
- Staples if available: faster, less precise

**Leave open when:** Bites (human or animal), wounds older than 6 hours, contaminated wounds, punctures, gunshot wounds, wounds over joints (tension causes breakdown).

**Dressing:**
Non-stick sterile if available. Change every 24–48 hours. Assess for infection at every change.

**Infection signs (develop 24–72 hours):**
Increasing redness beyond wound margins. Warmth. Swelling. Purulent discharge. Red streaks tracking up limb (lymphangitis = emergency – antibiotics needed immediately). Fever. Increasing pain after initial improvement.
