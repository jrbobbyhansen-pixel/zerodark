# Water Procurement – Finding Water in Any Terrain

**Topographic signs:** Water flows to low ground. Follow drainage patterns downhill. Dry riverbeds and gullies are where water was; dig 1-3ft at outer bends.

**Vegetation indicators:** Cottonwood, willow, and sycamore indicate water within 10ft. Dense green vegetation in dry terrain = moisture below. Animals and insects converge on water at dawn/dusk.

## Collection Methods

### Solar Still
Dig 4ft hole, place cup at center, cover with clear plastic sheet, pebble in center to create drip point, seal all edges with soil – yields 1-2 cups per day in full sun.

### Transpiration Bag
Tie clear plastic bag over dense leafy branch (living plant), seal at base – yields 1-3 cups overnight.

### Dew Collection
Drag cloth over grass at dawn, wring out into container.

## Requirements

1L/day bare survival minimum. 2L/day moderate activity. 4L/day heavy exertion or heat.

## Dehydration Progression

Thirst – dark yellow urine – headache – dizziness – confusion – organ failure (3 days no water).

## Never Drink

Seawater (accelerates dehydration), urine, blood, alcohol.
