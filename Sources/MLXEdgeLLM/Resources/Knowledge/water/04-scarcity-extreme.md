# Water Discipline in Extreme Scarcity

When water is critically scarce, every milliliter counts.

**Conservation:**
Move only at dawn, dusk, or night. Rest in full shade during day. Breathe through nose (mouth breathing loses 25% more moisture). Minimize talking. Avoid alcohol and caffeine (diuretics). Eat minimally — digestion requires water.

**Prioritizing usage:**
Drinking first. Cooling (evaporative cooling from damp cloth on skin) second. Wound irrigation third. Food preparation last. Do not wash clothing until water is reliably available.

**Extracting water from unlikely sources:**
- Cacti: barrel cactus pulp can be squeezed but often causes nausea — last resort only
- Dew: possible 200–500mL per morning in humid areas
- Succulent plants: many have water-storing tissue — squeeze pulp through cloth
- Fish: fish flesh contains 60–80% water, can be squeezed for fluid. Fish blood is salty — avoid.
- Eyes of large animals: contain fluid, not salty

**Dehydration math:**
30% reduction in blood volume = shock. 10% body weight loss to dehydration = dangerous. 150lb person can afford to lose 1.5L before impairment, 7.5L before death.

**Severe dehydration treatment:**
If conscious: small frequent sips (100mL every 5 minutes). Oral rehydration solution. If unconscious: nothing by mouth — IV fluids only.
