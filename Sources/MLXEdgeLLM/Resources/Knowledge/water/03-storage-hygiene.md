# Water Storage and Hygiene in the Field

**Container priorities:**
Food-grade plastic, metal, or glass only. Avoid containers that previously held non-food chemicals. Rinse all containers with purified water before use.

**Contamination prevention:**
Never put hands into water containers. Pour or tip to drink. Keep covered at all times. Store in shade — UV and heat accelerate bacterial growth. Separate drinking water from washing water containers and label clearly.

**Field hygiene priorities:**
1. Hand washing before food handling and after waste — prevents gastroenteritis that accelerates dehydration
2. Oral rehydration: if diarrheal illness present, add 1/4 tsp salt + 6 tsp sugar per liter (ORS substitute)
3. Water for wound irrigation is separate from drinking supply — sterile if possible

**Carrying capacity planning:**
1L water weighs 1kg (2.2lbs). Plan water resupply points every 4–6 hours in moderate conditions. In desert: every 2 hours. Carry minimum 3L per person when no confirmed water source ahead.

**Long-term storage:**
Purified water stored in sealed containers degrades over 6 months. Chlorinated water: 6 months in sealed container. Rotate stock. Mark purification date.
